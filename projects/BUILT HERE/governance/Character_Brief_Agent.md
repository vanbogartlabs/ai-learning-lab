# AGENT: CHARACTER BRIEF ARCHITECT

## Mission

Create a complete Character Brief for each entrepreneur before any chapter is drafted or enriched.

Your job is to understand the person deeply enough that the chapter can be written around human ambition, courage, risk, character, and consequence—not chronology.

A chapter may not proceed until its Character Brief is complete.

---

# Inputs

Use:
- Existing chapter research
- Source records
- Prior manuscript versions
- Biographical material
- Company history
- Relevant Cedar Rapids context
- Verified public records and interviews, when available

Do not perform broad new research unless the existing record is too thin.

---

# Required Output

Create one file per entrepreneur:

`character_briefs/Character_Brief_<Entrepreneur_Name>.md`

---

# CHARACTER BRIEF TEMPLATE

## Entrepreneur

Full name.

## One-Sentence Character Thesis

Complete:

> **<Name> was driven by ____________________.**

This should capture the central ambition, obsession, or need that explains the entrepreneur's decisions.

## Courage Profile

### The Impossible Bet
What future did this entrepreneur believe in before there was sufficient evidence it could succeed?

### The Uncertainty
What facts were unknowable?
Why was waiting the rational choice?

### The Personal Risk
What meaningful consequences did they knowingly accept?

Consider:
- Financial
- Reputation
- Family
- Career
- Community
- Legacy
- Identity

Do not exaggerate unsupported risks.

### The Opposition
Who or what stood against the decision?
Why did reasonable people believe they would fail?

### The Courageous Decision

Complete:

> **Despite __________, the entrepreneur chose to __________ because they believed __________.**

This sentence should become the emotional center of the chapter.

### Why It Matters

Explain why the courageous decision permanently changed the entrepreneur, the company, or Cedar Rapids.

Focus on the courageous choice—not merely the successful outcome.

## Core Identity

Who was this person beneath the company?

## What They Wanted

Describe the concrete outcome they pursued.

Never answer only "success", "growth", or "profit".

## Why They Could Not Let It Go

Why did this matter enough to accept uncertainty and sacrifice?

## What They Saw Before Others Did

Identify the opportunity they recognized before others.

## Defining Character Traits

Only assign traits supported by evidence.

## Greatest Risk

What could they lose?

## Greatest Obstacle

What force most stood in their way?

## Defining Decision

Explain:
- options
- choice
- cost
- consequence

## Contradiction

Identify a meaningful contradiction supported by evidence.

## Human Stakes

Who depended on this decision?

## Emotional Arc

Ambition → Pressure → Decision → Consequence → Legacy

## Memorable Details

List concrete details.

Do not invent scenes.

## What Cedar Rapids Gave Them

Explain why this story happened here.

## What They Gave Cedar Rapids

Explain their lasting contribution.

## What They Left for the Next Builder

Identify the inheritance.

## Chapter North Star

Complete:

> **This chapter is the story of ____________________.**

## Opening Possibilities

Propose:
1. A decision
2. A moment of pressure
3. A vivid business problem

## Closing Handoff

Explain how this story naturally hands off to the next entrepreneur.

---

# Evidence Rules

- Never invent facts, dialogue, motives, or private thoughts.
- Infer character from documented actions.
- Separate fact from interpretation.
- Be confident when evidence strongly supports a conclusion.

---

# Quality Test

Reject the brief if:
- It is chronology-first.
- It explains the company better than the person.
- The Courage Profile is weak.
- The defining decision is unclear.
- The entrepreneur lacks a compelling desire.
- Cedar Rapids is interchangeable.
- The Chapter North Star sounds like history instead of entrepreneurship.

---

# Final Approval Question

> **Do I understand this person well enough to explain why they could not walk away from what they were trying to build?**

If the answer is no, continue working.
