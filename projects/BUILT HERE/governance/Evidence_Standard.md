# Evidence Standard

**Project:** *Built Here: The Cedar Rapids Advantage*  
**Status:** Draft v1.0

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum evidentiary standard for every factual claim, interpretation, quotation, timeline entry, chapter, and conclusion in the project.

The objective is not simply to collect information.

The objective is to build a manuscript whose conclusions are traceable, reproducible, and intellectually defensible.

---

# 1. Core Principle

**Evidence precedes narrative.**

No story, argument, or interpretation may require unsupported facts.

If evidence is weak, the prose must reflect that uncertainty.

---

# 2. Source Hierarchy

## Tier 1 — Preferred

- Original archival documents
- Government records
- Court records
- Census records
- Company records
- SEC filings
- Contemporary correspondence
- Contemporary newspapers
- Recorded speeches
- Original photographs with provenance
- Contemporary financial reports

## Tier 2 — Strong

- Scholarly books
- Peer-reviewed articles
- University publications
- Well-documented biographies
- Major investigative journalism
- Museum collections

## Tier 3 — Supporting

- Local histories
- Trade publications
- Industry magazines
- Company histories
- Credible documentaries

## Tier 4 — Use With Caution

- Memoirs
- Oral histories
- Later recollections
- Unverified interviews
- Unsourced web articles
- Civic promotional material

Tier 4 sources should not independently establish important historical claims.

---

# 3. Corroboration Rules

Require corroboration for:

- dates
- financial figures
- quotations
- causal claims
- controversial events
- reputational claims
- statistics
- historical "firsts"

Exceptional claims require multiple independent sources whenever practical.

---

# 4. Confidence Levels

Every important claim should be classified:

- **High** — Multiple reliable sources agree.
- **Moderate** — Good evidence with limited corroboration.
- **Low** — Plausible but incomplete evidence.
- **Unresolved** — Conflicting or insufficient evidence.

Low-confidence claims should not be presented as established fact.

---

# 5. Conflicting Evidence

Agents must never silently choose one source.

Instead:

- document the conflict
- evaluate reliability
- explain the preferred interpretation
- preserve dissenting evidence

---

# 6. AI Prohibitions

Agents must never invent:

- dialogue
- quotations
- motives
- thoughts
- scene details
- chronology
- statistics
- relationships
- citations
- causal conclusions

When evidence is incomplete, say so.

---

# 7. Quotations

Direct quotations must:

- match the original wording
- preserve context
- identify the source
- identify the speaker
- identify the date when available

Do not modernize quotations without explicit notation.

---

# 8. Interpretations

Interpretation is encouraged.

Speculation is not.

Interpretive statements should be clearly distinguishable from factual statements.

---

# 9. Traceability

Every major factual claim must trace back to:

- one or more Source_Record artifacts
- supporting repository evidence
- research question(s)

No orphan facts.

---

# 10. Chapter Exit Criteria

A chapter cannot advance to final approval unless:

- material claims are sourced
- important claims meet confidence requirements
- unresolved disputes are documented
- quotations verified
- chronology verified
- evidence links complete

---

# Governing Principle

Readers should be able to trust the book not because AI produced it, but because every meaningful claim can be followed back to its evidence.
