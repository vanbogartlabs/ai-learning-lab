# Authority_Framework.md

## Purpose

The Authority Framework defines what each responsibility is permitted to do within the Publishing Operating System.

Authority belongs to responsibilities—not agents, models, prompts, or software.

Every responsibility must explicitly declare its authority level.

Authority is never implied.

---

# First Principle

> Authority exists to protect trust, not to increase automation.

The POS intentionally limits authority.

A responsibility should possess only the minimum authority required to fulfill its mission.

---

# Authority Hierarchy

The Publishing Operating System recognizes five authority levels.

Each level includes every authority below it.

## Level 1 — Inform
Observe, record, and report. No recommendations or modifications.

## Level 2 — Review
Evaluate, score, recommend, and flag issues. Cannot require changes.

## Level 3 — Challenge
Formally dispute work and present counterarguments. Cannot make final decisions.

## Level 4 — Block
Prevent workflow progression until objective blocking conditions are resolved.

## Level 5 — Approve
Advance workflow, resolve debates within scope, or approve content where authority exists.

---

# Constitutional Exception

The Human Author possesses ultimate authority and may override any responsibility.

---

# Scope Limitation

Authority is always scoped.

Examples:

- Reader Advocate may block chapter progression but may not rewrite chapters.
- Debate Judge may resolve debates but may not rewrite manuscripts.
- Orchestrator may advance workflow but may not approve editorial content.

---

# Separation of Authority

- Execution creates.
- Governance protects.
- Perspective challenges.

Responsibilities may not cross these boundaries except through explicit orchestration.

---

# Blocking Rules

Blocking authority requires objective criteria such as:

- Missing citations
- Preservation Rule violations
- High Reader Risk
- Repository inconsistency
- Failed QA thresholds

Subjective preference is never sufficient.

---

# Conflict Resolution

Review → Challenge → Debate → Judge → Human (if required)

# Authority Audit

Every exercise of authority produces a permanent artifact (e.g., Review_Report, Challenge_Report, Block_Record, Approval_Record, Debate_Record).

---

# Authority Replacement Rule

Authority belongs to responsibilities, not implementations. Replacing a model or agent does not change authority.

---

# Closing Principle

> Authority is the operating system's promise that important decisions are made intentionally, transparently, and by the correct responsibility.

The Publishing Operating System trusts responsibilities because their authority is explicit, limited, auditable, and accountable.
