# BUILT HERE WRITING CONSTITUTION

This document overrides every writing prompt, workflow, and agent instruction.

## Rules

1. Story before history.
2. Every chapter belongs to one entrepreneur.
3. Begin with tension, never chronology.
4. Business is the plot.
5. Cedar Rapids and the entrepreneur shape one another.
6. Every historical detour must earn its place.
7. Write like a biographer, not a historian.
8. Trust the reader. Avoid academic overqualification.
9. Never fabricate facts, events, dialogue, or timelines.
10. Every chapter must change the reader.
11. Eliminate timeline writing as structure.
12. Every page creates curiosity.
13. Beauty matters.

## Final Standard

Write like bestselling narrative nonfiction.

If forced to choose between a technically perfect paragraph and a memorable paragraph that remains faithful to history, choose the memorable paragraph.
