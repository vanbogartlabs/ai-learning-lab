# Failure_Modes.md

# BUILT HERE FAILURE MODES

Read this before writing or approving any chapter.

If ANY of these conditions exist, the chapter is rejected.

---

## AUTOMATIC FAILURES

### 1. History becomes the protagonist.
If the chapter is primarily about Cedar Rapids instead of one entrepreneur.

FAIL.

### 2. Timeline writing.
If the chapter advances because of dates instead of decisions.

FAIL.

### 3. No central business problem.
Every entrepreneur must be trying to solve something difficult.

FAIL.

### 4. No meaningful risk.
If nothing important can be lost, there is no story.

FAIL.

### 5. The entrepreneur disappears.
If more than two pages pass without the entrepreneur driving the narrative.

FAIL.

### 6. Academic tone.
If the writing sounds like a textbook, museum exhibit, dissertation, or Wikipedia article.

FAIL.

### 7. History lectures.
Background history may only appear when it directly explains the entrepreneur's challenge or decision.

FAIL.

### 8. Constant qualification.
Do not repeatedly interrupt the story with uncertainty, caveats, or evidence disclaimers.

Write confidently when supported by credible sources.

### 9. Emotionless prose.
If the reader feels no curiosity, tension, admiration, surprise, hope, loss, or triumph.

FAIL.

### 10. Forgettable chapter.
If a reader remembers the year but not the entrepreneur.

FAIL.

### 11. Weak ending.
Every chapter must make the reader want to continue.

FAIL.

### 12. Broken promise.
If the chapter no longer feels like the story of entrepreneurs who built Cedar Rapids.

FAIL.

---

# FINAL QUESTION

Would this chapter fit comfortably beside the best narrative nonfiction business biographies?

If not, rewrite it.
