# Chapter Quality Standard
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

This document defines what a great BUILT HERE chapter is.

It is the primary quality contract for every production agent. A chapter is not complete because it is accurate, well-written, or historically interesting. It is complete only when it achieves the objectives below.

---

# The Core Test

Every chapter must answer one question:

> Why is this entrepreneur unforgettable?

If the reader cannot answer that after one reading, the chapter fails.

---

# The Ten Standards

## 1. Reader Hook

Within the first 10% of the chapter, the reader must encounter the entrepreneur's most narratively significant, evidence-supported discovery as defined by the approved Narrative Discovery Record.

PASS:
- Impossible to miss.
- Changes how the reader views the entrepreneur.

FAIL:
- Buried.
- Omitted.
- Introduced too late without approved justification.

---

## 2. Courage

The chapter clearly explains:

- the decision,
- the uncertainty,
- the risk,
- why waiting was easier,
- why the entrepreneur acted anyway.

The reader should admire courage—not simply success.

---

## 3. Humanity

The entrepreneur must feel like a real person.

Required:
- limitations,
- setbacks,
- contradictions,
- difficult choices.

Forbidden:
- hero worship,
- flawless founders,
- motivational clichés.

---

## 4. Historical Integrity

Every material claim traces to evidence.

Interpretation is separated from fact.

Unknowns remain unknown.

---

## 5. Narrative Momentum

Every scene changes the reader's understanding.

Avoid chronology without purpose.

---

## 6. Cedar Rapids Contribution

The entrepreneur must change Cedar Rapids in some durable way.

The city is the setting, not the protagonist.

---

## 7. Inheritance

The chapter demonstrates what later builders inherited:

- people,
- institutions,
- capital,
- technology,
- confidence,
- permission,
- culture,
- opportunity.

---

## 8. Memory Test

Reader Agent should accurately answer:

- Who was this?
- What did they build?
- What surprised me?
- What decision mattered?
- Why should I care?

---

## 9. Bridge

The ending naturally points toward the next builder or era.

---

## 10. Commercial Quality

Someone with no connection to Cedar Rapids should still finish believing they learned something meaningful about entrepreneurship, leadership, or community.

---

# Automatic Failure Conditions

A chapter fails automatically if:

- the NDR astonishing fact is missing;
- unsupported facts appear;
- courage is unclear;
- the entrepreneur feels generic;
- the chapter could be moved to another entrepreneur with little change;
- the Reader Agent cannot identify the defining fact;
- Cedar Rapids is merely background decoration.

---

# Scoring Rubric

| Standard | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Reader Hook | 15 |
| Courage | 15 |
| Humanity | 10 |
| Historical Integrity | 15 |
| Narrative Momentum | 10 |
| Cedar Rapids Contribution | 10 |
| Inheritance | 5 |
| Memory Test | 10 |
| Bridge | 5 |
| Commercial Quality | 5 |

Bonus (up to +10): Emotional resonance without sacrificing accuracy.

Minimum passing score: 90/100.

---

# Final Question

Before locking any chapter, every reviewing agent must answer:

> If this became the only chapter a stranger ever read from BUILT HERE, would it make them want to read the rest of the book?

If the answer is no, the chapter is not ready.
