# Built Here Publishing Workflow v2

**Status:** Canonical research-to-publication workflow
**Purpose:** Produce the strongest possible finished manuscript with the fewest artifacts and handoffs necessary to protect narrative quality, historical integrity, and publication readiness.

## 1. Governing Principle

The workflow exists to improve the book. Repository activity, agent utilization, document count, research volume, and word count are not measures of success unless they produce a more accurate, compelling, coherent, and publishable manuscript.

When a workflow step does not change a decision, strengthen the evidence, improve the prose, protect the reader's trust, or prepare the approved manuscript for publication, omit it.

This document supersedes `governance/Book_Development_Workflow.md` as the canonical order of operations. It does not supersede the project's constitutional editorial documents or evidence rules. Authority remains, in order:

1. `The_Promise_of_Built_Here.md`
2. `Writing_Constitution_v2.md`
3. `Chapter_Contract_v2.md`
4. `Narrative_Test.md`
5. `Failure_Modes.md`
6. `Non_Negotiables.md`
7. Approved project-level decisions
8. This workflow
9. Specialist specifications and task prompts

## 2. Audit Conclusion

`Character_Brief_Agent.md`, `Chapter_Intent_Agent.md`, and `Narrative_Enrichment_Director.md` are necessary but not sufficient to produce a world-class narrative nonfiction book.

Together they solve three important problems:

- **Character Brief:** establishes who the entrepreneur is and why the reader should care.
- **Chapter Intent:** establishes why the chapter exists and which part of the entrepreneur's life belongs in it.
- **Narrative Enrichment:** improves the human presence, emotional force, rhythm, scenes, transitions, and literary quality of a structurally sound manuscript.

They do not, by themselves, ensure:

- that the central premise has survived serious challenge;
- that the selected entrepreneurs have enough person-level evidence to carry chapters;
- that scene-level evidence exists;
- that the chapters accumulate into one book rather than a profile collection;
- that claims and quotations remain traceable after rewriting;
- that the finished manuscript has received independent editorial, historical, legal, permissions, and production review;
- or that the exact publication candidate is the artifact that passed approval.

The existing repository contains most of these functions, but their sequence is fragmented. The primary need is not more agents. It is a single dependency-driven workflow that assigns one purpose to each existing artifact and places each review at the moment when it can still improve the book.

## 3. Workflow Gaps

### 3.1 Character viability occurs too late

The previous workflow approves a narrative architecture and table of contents before requiring a complete Character Brief. That allows a historical topic, company, institution, or era to receive a chapter slot before the record proves that a human protagonist can carry it.

**Correction:** Final chapter selection and the final table of contents must occur only after candidate entrepreneurs pass the Character Brief quality test. A thin record triggers targeted research, consolidation with another chapter, or removal. It never triggers invented psychology or an institution-centered substitute.

### 3.2 “Chapter Brief” is undefined and duplicates two better artifacts

The previous workflow requires a generic Chapter Brief while the current system also has a Character Brief, a Chapter Intent, and a Chapter Architect output containing many of the same fields.

**Correction:** Use only two planning artifacts:

- the **Character Brief**, which is person-level and reusable; and
- the **Chapter Intent**, which is chapter-level and selects the exact conflict, decision, evidence, exclusions, takeaway, and handoff.

“Chapter Brief” is no longer a separate canonical artifact. Where an older instruction says “Chapter Brief,” this workflow means the approved Character Brief plus the approved Chapter Intent.

### 3.3 Research captures claims better than narrative material

The data-first architecture is strong at sources, entities, events, dates, claims, and contradictions. World-class narrative nonfiction also needs verifiable human and physical detail: choices, relationships, letters, settings, tools, buildings, photographs, reported gestures, operating records, setbacks, and contemporaneous language.

**Correction:** Research must collect narrative-useful evidence as part of source and claim work. A scene may be planned only when time, place, participants, action, and material detail are supported. If those elements are absent, the writer uses transparent narrative summary rather than simulated scene work.

### 3.4 No explicit pre-draft evidence-readiness gate

An approved idea is not an evidence-ready chapter. Character ambition and a strong chapter promise can still rest on weak attribution, missing transaction records, or a company action not demonstrably controlled by the named entrepreneur.

**Correction:** Before drafting, verify the protagonist's agency, pivotal decision, stakes, outcome, essential scene details, material claims, quotations, conflicts, and source locations. Unsupported chapters return to targeted research or are redesigned before prose is written.

### 3.5 Chapters are locked before the book exists

The previous order allows final chapter approval before full manuscript assembly and whole-book review. That makes chapter-level approval conflict with later discoveries about repetition, pacing, sequence, missing escalation, duplicated protagonists, and the opening-to-ending arc.

**Correction:** Chapters may become **editorially ready**, but they are not finally locked until the assembled manuscript passes book-level structural review. Whole-book needs may reopen, combine, cut, reorder, or rewrite any chapter.

### 3.6 Fact verification is treated as one late stage

Verification only after editorial work is too late to prevent unsupported architecture, but verification only before editing cannot certify prose that has subsequently been rewritten.

**Correction:** Use two verification gates:

1. **Pre-draft evidence readiness** verifies that the planned story can be told.
2. **Post-enrichment manuscript verification** checks every material assertion and citation in the actual prose that will proceed toward publication.

Any substantive rewrite after the second gate reopens verification for changed passages.

### 3.7 Narrative enrichment lacks a precise position

The Narrative Enrichment Director says the research and architecture are complete, but older workflows do not state when structural editing ends and enrichment begins. Running enrichment too early beautifies material that may later be cut. Running it after final verification invalidates verification.

**Correction:** Narrative enrichment occurs after full-book structural, developmental, continuity, and repetition decisions are stable, but before final fact and citation verification, copyediting, and proof production.

### 3.8 No voice-calibration gate

Drafting every chapter before confirming voice can spread the same wrong tone across the manuscript.

**Correction:** Draft a small representative set before full production: an early historical chapter, an industrial or institutional chapter, and a modern chapter where possible. Approve the narrative distance, treatment of uncertainty, evidence-to-story balance, and literary register. Use those decisions to guide—not mechanically template—the remaining chapters.

### 3.9 Publication review is underspecified

Assembly and “publication” do not automatically provide legal review, sensitivity review, permissions, quotation clearance, notes, bibliography, AI disclosure, indexing, accessibility, print proofing, ebook validation, or final-candidate identity.

**Correction:** Publication readiness is a distinct sequence after the manuscript is editorially and factually stable. Final approval binds to an exact version and hash.

### 3.10 The workflow lacks an explicit stop rule for infrastructure

The repository can reward production of records and reports long after they cease improving the manuscript.

**Correction:** Stop research when the approved Character Brief, Chapter Intent, evidence-readiness gate, material claims, and publication risks are sufficiently answered. Do not create an artifact merely because an agent can produce it.

## 4. Redundancy and Scope Boundaries

### 4.1 Character Brief versus Chapter Intent

Some fields intentionally overlap, but their purposes differ:

- The Character Brief describes the person across the available record.
- The Chapter Intent selects the one business journey the chapter will tell.

The Character Brief owns identity, desire, character pattern, contradiction, enduring risk, and usable legacy. The Chapter Intent references that brief and owns the chapter promise, selected problem, selected decision, included and excluded evidence, emotional movement in this chapter, reader change, and actual next-chapter transition.

Do not copy the full Character Brief into the Chapter Intent.

### 4.2 Character Brief versus Chapter Architect

The Chapter Architect remains a useful function, but it does not produce a third planning document. It uses approved evidence and the Character Brief to produce or revise the Chapter Intent.

### 4.3 Chapter North Stars

The apparent duplicate has a legitimate distinction:

- Character Brief North Star: the enduring human story of the entrepreneur.
- Chapter Intent North Star: the one idea this particular chapter must leave with the reader.

If the two statements are identical, verify that the chapter is not trying to summarize an entire life instead of dramatizing one decisive journey.

### 4.4 Developmental, continuity, book-arc, and enrichment review

These reviews overlap only when their order and authority are unclear. Their canonical scopes are:

- **Developmental editing:** fixes the chapter's protagonist, conflict, structure, stakes, decision, outcome, and pacing.
- **Continuity editing:** fixes contradictions, repeated information, repeated lessons, entity treatment, chronology consistency, and handoffs across chapters.
- **Book-arc editing:** decides whether the opening asks the right question, the sequence earns the answer, emotional and thematic pressure accumulates, and the ending fulfills the beginning.
- **Narrative enrichment:** improves human presence, scenes supported by evidence, paragraph function, rhythm, memorable language, emotional force, and transitions after structural decisions are stable.

Do not ask all four functions to rewrite the same draft independently. Findings flow into one prioritized issue list, and one controlled revision becomes the next manuscript version.

### 4.5 Fact checking versus citation verification

These are distinct checks inside one verification gate:

- Fact checking asks whether the statement is true, fairly framed, appropriately qualified, and attributable to the named person.
- Citation verification asks whether the supporting source exists, resolves to the claim, supports the exact wording, contains the quotation at the stated location, and can be cited or used legally.

They may run in parallel, but their findings are resolved together before the gate passes.

### 4.6 Research functions

Source, entity, timeline, comparative, and claim work may run in parallel. Their structured records are complementary. Redundancy occurs when each produces a separate prose summary of the same material.

Store a fact once in canonical data. Generate views when useful. Write synthesis only when it changes thesis, character selection, chapter intent, or manuscript prose.

### 4.7 Approval gates

Routine chapter-by-chapter human approvals can freeze prose prematurely and make the book inconsistent. Human authority should concentrate on decisions that materially define or release the book. Chapter quality gates remain mandatory, but they may be enforced editorially by the Orchestrator unless the Project Owner requests direct review.

The canonical human gates are defined in Section 7.

## 5. Minimal Canonical Artifact Set

The workflow uses existing artifact types and creates no additional agent layer.

1. **Governing book documents** — promise, constitution, chapter contract, narrative tests, charter, editorial vision, evidence standard.
2. **Canonical research records** — sources, entities, events, claims, quotations, relationships, research questions, conflicts, and decisions.
3. **Character Brief** — one per unique entrepreneur or builder who remains a candidate protagonist.
4. **Chapter Intent** — one per proposed chapter.
5. **Manuscript prose** — versioned chapter drafts and assembled manuscripts.
6. **Unified issue record** — prioritized findings from developmental, continuity, book-arc, verification, legal, sensitivity, and production review; findings may live in existing review records rather than separate reports for every pass.
7. **Publication candidate and release package** — exact approved manuscript, notes, bibliography, disclosures, permissions status, index, formats, hashes, and archive.

No separate generic Chapter Brief is required. No duplicate research synthesis is required. No new artifact is created unless a downstream decision depends on it.

## 6. Canonical Research-to-Publication Workflow

### Stage 0 — Authority, reader promise, and publication target

**Purpose:** Define the book before generating work.

**Actions:**

- Confirm the central question, working thesis, reader promise, intended reader, ethical boundaries, geographic scope, and narrative mode.
- Test whether the premise is a real inquiry rather than civic mythology.
- Define the commercial and literary standard using comparable narrative nonfiction as calibration, not imitation.
- Decide the intended citation apparatus, treatment of quotations, AI disclosure principle, and publication formats early enough to capture the required metadata.

**Exit criteria:** The Project Owner approves the book promise, scope, thesis to be tested, intended reader experience, and definition of publication quality.

### Stage 1 — Research design and candidate universe

**Purpose:** Investigate the premise without prematurely assigning chapters.

**Actions:**

- Define bounded research questions.
- Build the candidate universe of entrepreneurs, companies, institutions, failures, exclusions, counterexamples, and peer cities.
- Identify which questions test the thesis and which merely add background.
- Establish source priorities and known archival, interview, permissions, or access risks.

**Exit criteria:** Research questions can disconfirm as well as support the thesis, candidate inclusion rules are explicit, and high-value source paths are identified.

### Stage 2 — Evidence acquisition and registration

**Purpose:** Create a reliable and narratively useful factual base.

**Actions:**

- Register sources with provenance and source-tier assessment.
- Deduplicate people, companies, institutions, and places.
- Build event records for chronology control, never for chapter structure.
- Build bounded claim records with supporting and contradicting sources.
- Register exact quotations with speaker, date, context, location, and permission status.
- Capture verifiable narrative material: consequential actions, letters, contracts, tools, buildings, photographs, operating records, relationships, reported gestures, physical constraints, setbacks, and outcomes.
- Run comparative research and preserve evidence against the thesis.

**Parallelism:** Source, entity, timeline, comparative, quote, and claim work may run concurrently when they use nonconflicting records.

**Exit criteria:** The evidence base can support a thesis assessment and identify which candidate entrepreneurs merit deeper person-level research.

### Stage 3 — Thesis assessment and protagonist viability

**Purpose:** Decide what the evidence permits the book to argue and who can carry it.

**Actions:**

- Evaluate the thesis, alternatives, counterexamples, exclusions, and limits.
- Rank candidate entrepreneurs by explanatory value, human stakes, documented agency, consequence, Cedar Rapids reciprocity, and evidence depth.
- Distinguish founder, owner, executive, creative builder, labor institution-builder, and institutional entrepreneur rather than silently treating them as identical.
- Reject any candidate whose chapter would actually belong to a city, company, institution, disaster, or era.

**Exit criteria:** The refined thesis is defensible, and every remaining candidate has a plausible person-driven story worth testing through a Character Brief.

### Stage 4 — Character Briefs and targeted character research

**Purpose:** Understand the person before assigning that person a final chapter.

**Actions:**

- Execute `Character_Brief_Agent.md` for each candidate.
- Separate documented action from supported interpretation and unresolved inference.
- Test desire, agency, traits, risk, obstacle, decision, contradiction, human stakes, emotional arc, memorable details, Cedar Rapids reciprocity, and legacy.
- For thin briefs, conduct only targeted research aimed at the missing character questions.
- If targeted research still cannot explain why the person could not walk away, remove the candidate, combine material under a defensible entrepreneur, or treat the material as supporting context. Do not force a chapter.

**Exit criteria:** Each retained protagonist has an approved Character Brief that passes the final approval question and offers enough evidence for emotional connection without invented interiority.

### Stage 5 — Provisional book architecture

**Purpose:** Build the book from viable human stories rather than historical coverage requirements.

**Actions:**

- Arrange the retained entrepreneurs so each chapter contributes new evidence toward the central answer.
- Define the opening question, escalation, reversals, recurring motifs, handoffs, and circular payoff.
- Identify repetitions, missing explanatory links, overrepresented industries, and weak stretches.
- Give historical periods space according to explanatory and narrative value, not equal coverage.
- Keep the architecture provisional until Chapter Intents prove each chapter necessary.

**Exit criteria:** A provisional sequence exists in which every proposed chapter has a viable protagonist and a distinct job in the whole book.

### Stage 6 — Chapter Intents and final table of contents

**Purpose:** Convert each viable character into one necessary chapter.

**Actions:**

- Execute `Chapter_Intent_Agent.md` for every proposed chapter.
- Reference the approved Character Brief rather than repeating it.
- Define the chapter promise, necessity, central business problem, pivotal decision, stakes, emotional movement, reader takeaway, required history, history to avoid, Cedar Rapids reciprocity, and transition.
- Name the evidence dependencies and scene possibilities tied to source or claim records.
- Review all Chapter Intents together for duplication, missing escalation, repeated lessons, inconsistent definitions of entrepreneur, and broken handoffs.
- Cut, combine, reorder, or redesign chapters that fail the Removal Test or Book Test.

**Exit criteria:** Every chapter can be described in one unforgettable sentence, has one central story, and changes the reader's understanding of the book's question. The Project Owner approves the final architecture and table of contents.

### Stage 7 — Chapter evidence readiness

**Purpose:** Prove that each approved intent can be written faithfully.

**Actions:**

- Resolve every material planned assertion to claim and source records.
- Verify that the pivotal decision belongs to the named protagonist.
- Confirm the options, stakes, consequences, chronology, and outcome.
- Verify quotations and scene details.
- Identify unresolved conflicts and decide whether to research, qualify, omit, or redesign.
- Confirm that required permissions or access issues will not invalidate essential material.
- Build the working claim map and citation map before drafting.

**Exit criteria:** The chapter has enough verified material to satisfy its Intent and Character Brief. If not, it returns to targeted research or architecture; it does not advance on confidence alone.

### Stage 8 — Voice calibration

**Purpose:** Establish one authorial standard before scaling production.

**Actions:**

- Draft a small representative set of chapters with different evidence conditions and eras.
- Test narrative distance, opening speed, treatment of uncertainty, scene-to-summary balance, business explanation, Cedar Rapids context, sentence rhythm, and emotional restraint.
- Compare the samples against the Promise, Constitution, Chapter Contract, Narrative Test, and Failure Modes.
- Record only decisions that guide subsequent prose; do not turn the samples into rigid templates.

**Exit criteria:** The Project Owner approves the narrative voice and evidence-to-story balance. Failed samples return to Intent, evidence readiness, or drafting as appropriate.

### Stage 9 — Controlled chapter drafting

**Purpose:** Produce complete narrative chapters from approved evidence and intent.

**Required inputs:** Approved Character Brief, Chapter Intent, evidence-ready claim and citation maps, verified quotations, and voice decisions.

**Actions:**

- Begin with documented tension, risk, decision, or opportunity.
- Keep business as the plot and the entrepreneur as the center of gravity.
- Use only historical context that changes the protagonist's problem or explains the consequence.
- Maintain the claim map as prose changes.
- Mark genuine open questions without inserting research-process language into reader-facing prose.
- Draft in parallel only when chapter dependencies, repeated context, and transitions are controlled.

**Exit criteria:** The draft fulfills the Chapter Intent, remains inside the evidence boundary, and passes the Chapter Contract, Narrative Test, and automatic Failure Modes.

### Stage 10 — Chapter developmental revision

**Purpose:** Fix the story before polishing sentences.

**Actions:**

- Compare the draft against the Character Brief and Chapter Intent.
- Repair protagonist disappearance, weak agency, absent conflict, low stakes, unclear business logic, chronology-driven structure, unsupported scenes, weak consequence, and dead endings.
- Remove information that does not increase story, character, tension, business understanding, Cedar Rapids reciprocity, emotion, or thesis.
- When a narrative weakness reflects missing evidence, return only that question to targeted research.
- When evidence cannot support the desired story, change or remove the story rather than decorating it.

**Exit criteria:** The chapter is editorally ready for assembly, not finally locked.

### Stage 11 — Rolling assembly and continuity control

**Purpose:** Prevent locally good chapters from becoming a repetitive or incoherent book.

**Actions:**

- Assemble chapters as soon as connected groups are ready.
- Check repeated facts, repeated lessons, inconsistent entities, terminology, chronology, voice, transitions, protagonist overlap, and explanatory gaps.
- Test whether each chapter answers one question, raises another, and strengthens the central thesis.
- Revise handoffs against the actual sequence, never a superseded table of contents.

**Exit criteria:** Every completed section reads as cumulative narrative rather than a set of independent profiles.

### Stage 12 — Full-manuscript structural and book-arc edit

**Purpose:** Make the assembled manuscript one inevitable story.

**Actions:**

- Evaluate the opening question, thesis progression, emotional escalation, pacing, motifs, reversals, chapter order, proportion, redundancy, and ending.
- Apply the Book Arc Editor and Narrative Continuity standards to the entire manuscript.
- Cut any chapter that survives only because work was invested in it.
- Combine duplicated journeys, reorder chapters, reopen Chapter Intents, and commission targeted research where the whole-book argument exposes a real gap.
- Ensure the ending answers the opening by completing the journey rather than summarizing it.

**Exit criteria:** The opening makes a clear promise, every chapter earns part of the answer, the final chapter produces an emotionally and intellectually satisfying payoff, and the Project Owner approves the manuscript's structure. Only now may chapter structure be considered stable.

### Stage 13 — Narrative enrichment

**Purpose:** Turn a structurally excellent, evidence-ready manuscript into a memorable reading experience.

**Actions:**

- Execute `Narrative_Enrichment_Director.md` on the stable manuscript.
- Strengthen human presence, supported scenes, decision moments, pressure, rhythm, paragraph movement, literary precision, emotional resonance, and transitions.
- Prefer scenes only when the record supports time, place, participants, action, and detail.
- Replace information-only paragraphs with purposeful prose or remove them.
- Treat the “two functions per paragraph” and “three beautiful moments” rules as editorial diagnostics, not quotas that encourage ornament or visible formula.
- Preserve clarity and factual meaning. Do not make uncertainty disappear when it is material.
- If enrichment requires facts the record does not contain, return to targeted research or write transparent summary. Never fabricate.

**Exit criteria:** Readers encounter memorable people rather than research summaries; the manuscript passes the Humanity and Beauty tests without becoming overwritten, sentimental, or synthetic.

### Stage 14 — One-author line and voice edit

**Purpose:** Remove the seams of multi-agent production.

**Actions:**

- Normalize narrative voice, distance, diction, rhythm, names, terminology, dates, and treatment of uncertainty.
- Remove AI habits: repeated framing, parallel chapter formulas, explicit moralizing, generic takeaways, excessive signposting, and conspicuous transition templates.
- Protect deliberate variation among chapters while maintaining one governing voice.

**Exit criteria:** The manuscript sounds authored rather than assembled and contains no avoidable stylistic pattern that exposes the production machinery.

### Stage 15 — Final factual and citation verification

**Purpose:** Certify the actual enriched and line-edited prose.

**Actions:**

- Verify every material factual assertion against canonical claims and sources.
- Verify attribution of every decision to the named protagonist.
- Confirm dates, numbers, causal language, corporate continuity, disputed accounts, living-person claims, and historical “firsts.”
- Verify every quotation against the original source and exact location.
- Confirm citation completeness, citation style, permissions status, and source-to-claim resolution.
- Correct prose, qualify claims, remove unsupported material, or reopen targeted research.
- Recheck any passage changed during resolution.

**Exit criteria:** Every material assertion is verified, appropriately qualified, or removed; every citation resolves; every quotation is exact and cleared for its intended use.

### Stage 16 — Independent manuscript review

**Purpose:** Test the work against readers and risks not fully visible inside production.

**Actions:**

- Obtain independent narrative/editorial review of pace, clarity, character attachment, and commercial readability.
- Obtain historical or subject-matter review of consequential claims and omissions.
- Conduct legal, privacy, sensitivity, fairness, and reputational review where living people, Indigenous history, race, labor conflict, accidents, exclusion, bankruptcy, environmental effects, or corporate conduct create risk.
- Use representative general readers to identify confusion, boredom, missing context, and moments where the manuscript's intended emotional effect does not occur.
- Consolidate findings into one prioritized issue list. Do not accept every suggestion automatically; resolve findings according to the governing promise and evidence.

**Exit criteria:** Material external findings are resolved, rejected with rationale, or disclosed. Substantive revisions return to the relevant editorial and verification gates.

### Stage 17 — Publication apparatus and copyedit

**Purpose:** Prepare an editorially stable manuscript for professional production.

**Actions:**

- Copyedit grammar, usage, consistency, house style, and mechanical accuracy without flattening voice.
- Finalize notes, bibliography, acknowledgements, AI disclosure, captions, credits, permissions, and index inputs.
- Verify names, titles, dates, cross-references, tables, links, and front and back matter.
- Confirm image quality, rights, placement, and captions if images are used.
- Create an exact publication-candidate version after copyediting.

**Exit criteria:** All text and supporting apparatus are complete, consistent, permission-cleared, and tied to the same manuscript version.

### Stage 18 — Production proof and final candidate QA

**Purpose:** Ensure the approved book survives conversion into reader-facing formats.

**Actions:**

- Produce print and digital proofs from one canonical source.
- Proofread the rendered pages, not only the source Markdown.
- Validate typography, page breaks, headings, notes, index locators, images, links, navigation, metadata, and accessibility.
- Verify EPUB behavior and print specifications.
- Run a final spot-check of factual corrections after typesetting.
- Record the exact file hashes of the candidate that passed QA.

**Exit criteria:** All formats represent the same approved text, contain no production defects, meet accessibility and metadata requirements, and have stable identifying hashes.

### Stage 19 — Final approval, release, and archive

**Purpose:** Publish the exact artifact that passed every gate.

**Actions:**

- Present the publication candidate, unresolved-risk statement, permissions status, verification status, and format proofs to the Project Owner.
- Obtain explicit release approval bound to exact hashes.
- Publish only the approved candidate.
- Archive source records, claim maps, review resolutions, permissions, proofs, release files, and prior manuscript versions without overwriting history.

**Exit criteria:** The Project Owner has approved the exact publication candidate, the release package is complete, and the authoritative archive can reproduce and defend the published book.

## 7. Canonical Human Approval Gates

Human attention is reserved for decisions that define or release the book:

1. **Book Promise Gate:** central question, intended reader, scope, thesis to test, ethical boundaries, and publication ambition.
2. **Architecture Gate:** refined thesis, approved Character Briefs, approved Chapter Intents, final table of contents, opening question, and ending concept.
3. **Voice Gate:** representative chapter samples establish the narrative voice and evidence-to-story balance.
4. **Full-Manuscript Gate:** the structurally edited manuscript has one coherent arc and is ready for enrichment and final verification.
5. **Publication Gate:** exact fact-checked, citation-verified, copyedited, permission-cleared, proofed files are approved for release.

A failed chapter quality check does not require a ceremonial human gate unless it changes the approved thesis, architecture, ethical treatment, or publication risk. It returns to the earliest stage capable of fixing the defect.

## 8. Repair Loops

Corrections move backward only as far as necessary:

- Missing source or unsupported claim → Stage 2 or 7.
- Thin or falsely attributed protagonist → Stage 3 or 4.
- Chapter has no necessary purpose → Stage 5 or 6.
- Draft lacks conflict, agency, or consequence → Stage 9 or 10.
- Repetition, broken sequence, or weak ending → Stage 11 or 12.
- Emotionally forgettable but structurally sound prose → Stage 13.
- Voice inconsistency or AI pattern → Stage 14.
- Factual or citation defect → Stage 15, followed by re-verification of the changed passage.
- External legal, sensitivity, or reader problem → Stage 16 and the earliest relevant repair stage.
- Copy or production defect → Stage 17 or 18.

Never regenerate unaffected artifacts. Preserve version history. A corrected artifact must be rechecked by every downstream gate invalidated by the change.

## 9. Safe Parallelism

Parallelism is encouraged only where it cannot fragment the book:

**Safe to parallelize:**

- source discovery and registration;
- entity, event, quote, and claim processing;
- bounded comparative research;
- targeted research for independent Character Brief gaps;
- chapter drafting after shared voice calibration and when dependencies do not overlap;
- fact and citation verification with separate scopes;
- format QA across outputs generated from the same candidate.

**Keep coordinated or sequential:**

- thesis decisions;
- protagonist selection;
- final architecture and chapter order;
- voice calibration;
- cross-chapter continuity;
- book-arc decisions;
- narrative enrichment across recurring motifs and transitions;
- one-author line editing;
- final issue resolution;
- release approval.

Parallel drafts are never assumed to form a coherent manuscript merely because each passes its own chapter test.

## 10. Stage-Level Quality Questions

Before advancing any chapter, ask:

1. Is the protagonist a documented person with agency in the defining decision?
2. Does the Character Brief explain what this person wanted and why the pursuit endured?
3. Does the Chapter Intent give this chapter one indispensable job?
4. Can the planned story be told from verified evidence without simulated scenes or interiority?
5. Is business the plot rather than background?
6. Do the stakes matter to people beyond an abstract company?
7. Does Cedar Rapids materially shape the decision, and does the decision alter Cedar Rapids?
8. Does the chapter add something the surrounding chapters do not?
9. Does the ending create the actual next chapter?
10. Would a reader unfamiliar with Cedar Rapids care enough about the person to continue?

Before advancing the manuscript, ask:

1. Does the opening establish the question the ending answers?
2. Does every chapter earn part of that answer?
3. Does tension, consequence, and meaning accumulate rather than reset?
4. Are repeated protagonists, facts, lessons, and motifs controlled?
5. Does the book sound like one author?
6. Is the prose memorable without sacrificing clarity or evidence?
7. Are difficult histories integrated honestly rather than used as context decoration?
8. Would removing any chapter weaken both the argument and the reading experience?
9. Has the actual final prose—not an earlier draft—passed verification?
10. Is this competitive with excellent published narrative nonfiction rather than merely good for an AI workflow?

## 11. Definition of Complete

The project is complete only when:

- the central question is answered by the accumulated stories rather than asserted in exposition;
- every included chapter has a viable Character Brief and Chapter Intent;
- every chapter centers one documented entrepreneurial builder and satisfies the Chapter Contract;
- the manuscript reads as one escalating story with an earned ending;
- narrative enrichment has improved human presence and literary quality without invention;
- the exact final prose has passed fact and citation verification;
- independent editorial, historical, legal, sensitivity, and reader findings have been resolved;
- copy, notes, bibliography, permissions, AI disclosure, indexing, metadata, and accessibility are complete;
- print and digital proofs represent the same approved candidate;
- release approval is bound to exact file hashes;
- and the Project Owner judges the book worthy of publication.

A complete draft is not a complete book. A valid repository is not a complete book. The workflow ends only when the finished artifact earns the reader's trust, attention, memory, and recommendation.
