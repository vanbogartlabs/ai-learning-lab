## Scope, Authority, Evidence Posture, and RQ Map

This successor applies the approved `PRODUCTION_EVIDENCE_POLICY.md` to frozen Chapter 32. It does not convert Chris Cuddy's title or public quotation into personal approval authority. The strongest supportable chapter is a corporate-decision story with a named executive voice: ADM idled dry-mill ethanol production, redirected parts of its corn-processing system toward other products, and announced furloughs; Cuddy led the relevant business domain and publicly described the actions as difficult. Architecture controls the chapter's function, not the allocation of historical authority.

Mapped questions: **RQ-011, RQ-014, RQ-015, RQ-020, RQ-031, RQ-033, RQ-034, RQ-041, RQ-043, and RQ-045**.

## Executive Candidate, Corporate Agency, and Chapter Necessity

- **Qualified named figure:** Christopher M. (Chris) Cuddy, then ADM senior vice president and president of Carbohydrate Solutions.
- **Documented domain:** SEC filings place wet and dry corn milling, ethanol, industrial alcohol, starches, and Vantage Corn Processors within or across the business structure Cuddy led.
- **Strongest supportable corporate action:** in April 2020 ADM announced temporary dry-mill ethanol idling in Cedar Rapids, product redirection toward uses including industrial alcohol/hand sanitizer and containerboard starch, and about ninety Cedar Rapids furloughs.
- **Strongest supportable person-level action:** Cuddy publicly characterized the actions as “very difficult decisions” and spoke from a relevant executive domain. This establishes public/professional accountability, not that he proposed, approved, signed, or held final authority over any component.
- **Agency form:** institutional/corporate action with distributed and undisclosed internal decision rights; Cuddy is a responsible public representative, not a verified sole decider.
- **Chapter necessity:** show that Cedar Rapids' inherited processing capability could be reallocated under shock, while the authority to reallocate sat inside a global corporation and the immediate burden reached local workers.

## Defining Corporate Decision, Responsibility, and Safer Alternatives

> **In April 2020, ADM temporarily idled Cedar Rapids dry-mill ethanol production, redirected parts of its processing response toward other products, and announced about ninety local furloughs; Chris Cuddy, the executive leading the relevant business unit, publicly called the actions difficult without claiming personal approval.**

This is the qualified production center. Downstream prose must retain “ADM” as the acting subject for idling, redirection, and furlough decisions unless later evidence allocates a specific act to a person.

### Supported responsibility and exposure

Cuddy's officer and segment roles place him within the relevant management domain, and his quotation attached his professional standing to the company's explanation and stated employee-care terms. That supports public accountability and a role in communicating corporate responsibility. It does not establish personal financial exposure, a delegated approval threshold, a signature, a plant directive, or final responsibility for dry-mill, wet-mill, customer-allocation, or staffing choices.

At the institution level, ADM accepted operating and market consequences from idling production and changing product emphasis. Workers accepted no comparable corporate option; furlough transferred immediate uncertainty to households. Exact worker outcomes remain unknown.

### Structurally safer alternatives

ADM could analytically have continued output into weak gasoline demand, reduced both mills, idled more capacity, selected a different product mix, delayed staffing action, or shifted production elsewhere. No inspected record sets out the actual alternatives or attributes their evaluation to Cuddy. These alternatives may frame the business problem but not a reconstructed deliberation.

## Responsible Narrative Spine

1. **Inherited capability:** Cedar Rapids has distinct corn-processing assets, workforce, transport, and product pathways.
2. **External shock:** weak fuel demand creates pressure that ADM publicly ties to the pandemic.
3. **Corporate allocation:** ADM idles one process, redirects part of its production response, and changes staffing.
4. **Named accountability without invented authority:** Cuddy, who leads the relevant business domain, publicly calls the actions difficult; the chapter states that the record does not show he approved them.
5. **Unequal consequence:** the corporation can rebalance a portfolio; about ninety Cedar Rapids workers receive furlough notices while retaining announced benefits.
6. **Inheritance:** Cedar Rapids keeps adaptable industrial capability, but local workers and the city do not necessarily control how a global owner assigns it.

This spine makes the authority gap part of the chapter's meaning instead of hiding it behind a heroic executive narrative.

## Narratable Claims

- ADM, not Cuddy personally, announced and carried out the idling, redirection, and staffing actions described in the inspected record.
- Cuddy held a senior officer and business-unit leadership role relevant to the affected operations.
- He publicly described the corporate actions as difficult.
- His public role can be described as professional accountability or representative responsibility, with an explicit qualifier that approval authority is not established.
- Different process and product pathways gave ADM options during weak fuel demand.
- Furlough notices exposed Cedar Rapids workers to immediate uncertainty, while continued medical benefits and unemployment eligibility were announced mitigations.
- Cedar Rapids supplied useful industrial capability without controlling the corporate allocation.

## Unknowns and Prohibited Claims

- Do not write that Cuddy decided, ordered, approved, signed, proposed, or personally selected the Cedar Rapids mill split.
- Do not make a local plant manager, ADM CEO, executive committee, or board the decider without an attributable record.
- Do not infer that Cuddy accepted personal financial risk or knowingly rejected a named alternative.
- Do not invent forecasts, margins, customer orders, grind volumes, internal models, approval meetings, notification scenes, dialogue, or private emotion.
- Do not claim that all redirected grind occurred at the Cedar Rapids wet mill or that all sanitizer/containerboard output was produced there.
- Do not collapse dry-mill idling, wet-mill operation, product routing, marketing, and furlough authority into one decision right.
- Do not claim all workers returned, lost their jobs, suffered a particular household outcome, or experienced the announced benefits identically.
- Do not turn ADM's stated rationale into independently proved sole causation.
- Do not describe a temporary announcement as a fully traced long-term outcome.

## Supported Interpretation

ADM's response shows that industrial continuity is not sameness. A city may retain plants and skill while products, operating intensity, and employment are reallocated by an owner with a wider portfolio. The chapter's strongest contrast is between corporate option and worker constraint.

Cuddy's presence matters because it places a named public executive inside the relevant domain and makes corporate accountability visible. His presence must not cure the missing decision-rights evidence. A responsible chapter can state both facts at once: he was not a random spokesperson, and the current record does not establish that he approved the measures he described.

## Cedar Rapids Exchange

### What Cedar Rapids gave ADM

Cedar Rapids supplied corn-processing facilities, grain access, transportation, utilities, regulatory infrastructure, and a workforce capable of supporting ethanol, industrial alcohol, starch, and related production. Those accumulated capabilities made multiple product responses physically possible.

### What ADM gave and withheld

ADM supplied outside markets, corporate capital, operating systems, and the ability to redirect some production. It also exercised corporate options that exposed about ninety local workers to furlough. Cedar Rapids retained part of a useful processing system but did not hold the final allocation authority documented in the current packet.

## Conflicts and Tensions to Preserve

1. **Relevant domain versus specific authority:** Cuddy led the business unit; the record does not identify his approval.
2. **Adaptation versus interruption:** redirection and idling happened in the same response.
3. **Corporate flexibility versus household constraint:** portfolio options were not shared equally with workers.
4. **Food-system inheritance versus industrial end uses:** one corn platform served fuel, sanitizer/industrial alcohol, and packaging starch.
5. **Public statement versus internal chain:** a named voice does not reveal proposal, review, signature, or final decision rights.
6. **Temporary measure versus durable consequence:** announced duration and later individual outcomes remain incomplete.
