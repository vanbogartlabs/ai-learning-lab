# Research Record — Chapter 32: Food City, Still

## Scope, Authority, Evidence Posture, and RQ Map

This record tests the frozen Chapter 32 slot without treating the approved architecture as historical evidence. It asks who had authority over ADM's April 2020 Cedar Rapids dry-mill idling, wet-mill product redirection, and associated staffing consequences. Historical Chapter 32 is an evidence summary only. The controlling research standard is person-level attribution, not title plus publicity.

Mapped questions: **RQ-011, RQ-014, RQ-015, RQ-020, RQ-031, RQ-033, RQ-034, RQ-041, RQ-043, and RQ-045**.

## Entrepreneur Candidate, Agency, and Chapter Necessity

- **Candidate:** Christopher M. (Chris) Cuddy.
- **Documented role:** ADM's 2020 Form 10-K identifies Cuddy as an ADM senior vice president since May 2015 and president of the Carbohydrate Solutions business unit since March 2015. The filing places wet and dry corn milling, industrial alcohol, ethanol, and starches inside that segment and Vantage Corn Processors inside it as a subsegment.
- **Public attribution:** ADM's release, reproduced by the Corridor Business Journal, quotes Cuddy calling the April 2020 actions “very difficult decisions.” It identifies him as president of Carbohydrate Solutions.
- **Agency limit:** Neither the inspected release nor the 10-K says that Cuddy personally proposed, approved, or held final delegated authority for the Cedar Rapids operating, product-mix, or furlough decisions. “These are ... decisions” is a collective corporate statement, not a decision-rights record.
- **Chapter necessity:** The slot is necessary because late-book industrial continuity must confront outside ownership and unequal options: a global company could idle one Cedar Rapids process, redirect another, and transfer immediate uncertainty to workers. That function still lacks a verified person-level protagonist.

## Defining Decision, Risk, and Safer Alternative

### Candidate decision

> In April 2020, ADM temporarily idled dry-mill ethanol production in Cedar Rapids while directing corn grind toward other products, including industrial alcohol/hand-sanitizer uses and starch for containerboard, and furloughed about ninety Cedar Rapids employees.

This is a documented **ADM corporate decision**. It is not yet a documented **Cuddy decision**.

### Supported exposure and responsibility

Cuddy's business-unit presidency and senior-vice-president status place him inside the relevant management chain. His public statement attached his professional standing to the response and to employee care. The 10-K shows that Carbohydrate Solutions was a material reportable segment and that the dry-mill business sat within it.

The record does not establish Cuddy's personal financial exposure, who signed the furlough authorization, whether Cedar Rapids plant management recommended the split, whether ADM's CEO or an executive committee approved it, or who decided the destination and quantity of redirected grind. Corporate accountability is plausible; personally attributable decision authority remains unproved.

### Structurally safer alternatives

For the corporation, alternatives included continuing production into weak gasoline demand, reducing output at both mills, idling more capacity, shifting a different product mix, or delaying staffing action. For Cuddy personally, the record does not establish an available safer course because it does not establish his decision right. None of the sources records management's alternative analysis.

## Supported Interpretation

ADM's response demonstrates industrial adaptability under corporate ownership: a company with multiple facilities and product pathways could stop one process while redirecting another. It also demonstrates asymmetry. ADM held a portfolio of options; furloughed workers faced household consequences without comparable diversification.

Cuddy is a relevant executive and more than a random spokesperson. The SEC filing confirms that he led the segment containing the affected businesses. That still does not answer the assignment's authority question. A senior title identifies a probable decision domain; it does not prove who exercised a specific decision right in April 2020.

## Cedar Rapids Exchange

### What Cedar Rapids gave ADM

Cedar Rapids supplied two distinct corn-processing facilities, grain access, transportation, utilities, regulatory infrastructure, and a workforce capable of supporting ethanol, industrial alcohol, starch, and related processing. These accumulated capabilities made more than one product response physically possible.

### What ADM gave and withheld

ADM supplied outside markets, corporate capital, operating systems, and the ability to redirect some local production. It also exercised corporate options that exposed about ninety Cedar Rapids workers to furlough. The city received continued usefulness at part of the system but did not control the corporate allocation. No source supports claiming that Cedar Rapids government or workers shared final product-mix authority.

## Conflicts and Tensions to Preserve

1. **Relevant executive versus proved decider:** Cuddy led the business unit, but no inspected record allocates this decision to him.
2. **Adaptability versus interruption:** Wet-mill redirection and dry-mill idling occurred together.
3. **Corporate option versus worker constraint:** ADM could rebalance assets; workers could not rebalance households on the same terms.
4. **Food-city identity versus industrial products:** The same corn platform served ethanol, sanitizer/industrial alcohol, and packaging starch.
5. **Company rationale versus full causal proof:** Weak gasoline demand was ADM's stated rationale; internal models, customer orders, and approval materials were not recovered.
6. **Temporary announcement versus later outcome:** The release anticipated at least four months; the inspected packet does not trace every worker or restart decision.

## Human Stakes and Consequences

About ninety Cedar Rapids employees were notified of temporary furlough. Continued medical benefits and unemployment eligibility reduced but did not erase uncertainty. Remaining employees, managers, suppliers, carriers, and public systems had to support a changed production mix. Exact household, supplier, production, and restart outcomes remain unknown.

## Chapter Necessity

Chapter 32 is necessary to puncture the idea that industrial continuity means unchanged products, owners, or employment. It must show that inherited physical capability remains useful only through renewed allocation—and that the power to allocate is not equally held. The chapter cannot satisfy the people-first constitution until the named decider is verified.

## Inheritance from Chapter 31 and Transition to Chapter 33

- **Incoming condition:** Chapter 31 is intended to leave staged-risk infrastructure: shared space can preserve a first experiment and future choice. No personal handoff to ADM may be implied.
- **Chapter 32 transformation if resolved:** The scale changes from a survivable merchant test to allocation across industrial assets and workers under outside corporate ownership.
- **Outgoing condition:** Cedar Rapids retains adaptable industrial capability but not final local control. Chapter 33 receives that control problem in aerospace, where technical capability must be assigned consequential work by a wider corporation.
- **Transition limit:** Do not claim Nathan Boelkins learned from, met, or inherited authority from Cuddy.
