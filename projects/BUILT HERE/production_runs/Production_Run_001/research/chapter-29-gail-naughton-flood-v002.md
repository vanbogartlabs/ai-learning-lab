## Responsible Narrative Spine

1. **Unknown loss:** the building is inaccessible; neither readers nor institutional leaders may use later knowledge to resolve the moment prematurely.
2. **Collective emergency work:** two semi-truck loads had been removed by “we,” and board and staff were meeting on disaster recovery. Keep the actors plural.
3. **Named public commitment:** Naughton, as president/CEO, publicly says the cultural mission will continue and the institution will rebuild.
4. **Bounded consequence:** the commitment creates a public obligation and makes institutional continuity narratable; it does not prove that her words caused the meetings, salvage, or later recovery.
5. **Unresolved work:** collections, staff responsibility, access, and recovery remain unsettled. Chapter 30 receives this condition and begins only when rebuilding can be narrated as an attributed institutional program.

This spine is responsible because it does not require a fabricated authorization, private motive, or victory forecast.
