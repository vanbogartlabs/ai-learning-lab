## Defining Institutional Action, Responsibility, and Safer Alternatives

> **In 2025 and 2026, Collins Aerospace/RTX secured major Black Hawk avionics and FAA radar awards connected publicly to Cedar Rapids capability; Nathan Boelkins and Jenny Miller supplied named leadership and program voices, but the record does not identify who authorized the bids, investments, products, or local work allocation.**

This is the qualified production center. The “decision” that may safely appear is the corporation's commitment to pursue and perform awarded work, described institutionally. No source currently supports turning that commitment into “Boelkins decided” or “Miller chose.”

### Supported responsibility and exposure

Boelkins's Avionics presidency establishes broad public and professional responsibility for a large portfolio. Miller's role and product explanation establish program-adjacent public accountability. That is structural professional exposure. The inspected record does not establish that either person personally accepted bid loss, capital, schedule, staffing, site, or product downside. It also does not disclose personal financial exposure or the internal allocation of decision rights.

### Structurally safer alternatives

At corporate level, Collins/RTX could analytically decline to bid, offer a different architecture, defer investment, allocate work to another site, buy capability, or rely on legacy products. No inspected source records Boelkins or Miller evaluating or rejecting those alternatives. They may frame the business stakes only as analytical possibilities.
