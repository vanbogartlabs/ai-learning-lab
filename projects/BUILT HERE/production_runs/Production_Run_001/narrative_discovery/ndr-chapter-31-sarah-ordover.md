# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 31: Sarah Ordover

## The One Unforgettable Truth

Before a merchant could test one small stall, Sarah Ordover publicly led a nonprofit commitment to secure the flood-affected Quality Chef site, preserve threatened industrial buildings, and carry the larger place-and-platform risk.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: High for Ordover's documented 2010 public role; qualified for the final form.** Contemporary reporting identifies her as Cedar Rapids City Market president and supports the site request, preservation commitment, proposed $5.8 million plan, and site-to-campaign-to-construction sequence. The board, City, consultants, funders, and later operators retained independent agency; the initial plan differed from the completed market.

## Why It Changes Understanding

The market's “small first bet” was not naturally low-risk. It existed because a nonprofit coalition first concentrated land, preservation, funding, construction, and public-accountability exposure. Ordover makes that upstream platform wager visible.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

Abandoning the site, accepting demolition, leasing ordinary space, or waiting for private development was structurally safer. Ordover instead accepted public sequencing and execution responsibility before site control or capital was settled. Builders should care because staged entry for many depends on someone carrying infrastructure risk first.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

Ordover carried the building-scale wager that later let entrepreneurs begin with a stall-sized bet.

## Mandatory Placement

State the platform-before-stall truth on the opening page and within the first 10 percent. Put the 2010 site decision before opening-period merchants or incubator outcomes.

## Omission Risk

End exactly: **shared place, gathered traffic, selection, utilities, programming, and smaller spaces allow entrepreneurs to test products without first carrying a whole storefront.** The blocked Chapter 32 receives only a city practiced in adaptive use and changing productive forms.
