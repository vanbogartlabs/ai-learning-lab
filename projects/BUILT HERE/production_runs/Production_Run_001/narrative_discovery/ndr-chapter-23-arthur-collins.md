# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 23: Arthur Collins

## The One Unforgettable Truth

Collins Radio did not have one clean founding moment: Arthur Collins moved specialized radio work through successive thresholds—laboratory activity in 1931, use of the Collins Radio name by late 1932, a Delaware charter in 1933, and Iowa reincorporation in 1937—turning personal capability into an entity with obligations.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate/qualified.** Institutional and retrospective sources consistently associate Collins with early radio work and the company bearing his name; repository chronology supports the four formation thresholds. The corporate archive identifies customer, bid, receipt, employee, financial, and legal record classes. Original filings, capitalization, ownership, loans, guarantees, and early results remain unverified.

## Why It Changes Understanding

The staggered formation prevents a genius-in-a-workshop origin myth. Technical skill became durable through ordinary commercial and legal work—selling, collecting, bidding, employing, recording, and accepting obligations—not through one invention or ceremonial date.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

Remaining an amateur, consultant, or employee, limiting work to personal capacity, or delaying formalization was structurally safer. Collins instead made his professional name answerable for an organization during contraction. Builders should care because capability becomes inheritable only when routines and obligations can outlast one person’s hands.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

Arthur Collins founded a company more than once because each new legal and commercial threshold made his radio skill less personal and more durable.
