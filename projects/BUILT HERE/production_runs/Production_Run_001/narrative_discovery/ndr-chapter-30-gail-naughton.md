# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 30: Gail Naughton

## The One Unforgettable Truth

Under Gail Naughton's leadership, NCSML preserved its historic building by moving it and preserved institutional wholeness by separating programs, collections, capital, offices, and construction into workstreams that could move on different schedules.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate to high for attributed Chapter 30 leadership.** Official NCSML sources support more than $28 million raised, the original building saved and moved, 30,000 square feet added, displaced programming, and July 2012 reopening. Distributed authority, restricted uses, exact move/elevation measurements, and personal financial exposure remain bounded or unresolved.

## Why It Changes Understanding

Preservation was not stasis. Movement and operational separation made continuity possible. The apparent paradox embodies Naughton's institutional entrepreneurship without making her owner, sole governor, conservator, engineer, fundraiser, or builder.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

Waiting for one permanent answer, repairing in place, abandoning the historic structure, or reducing the mission could have lowered coordination risk. Naughton instead kept public function alive while leading a permanent rebuild through many independent actors. Builders should care because continuity can require decomposing a damaged whole before recombining it.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

Naughton helped save the museum by moving its building and letting every part of the institution recover on its own clock.
