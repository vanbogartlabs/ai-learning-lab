# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 21: Mary Snyder

## The One Unforgettable Truth

When Depression-era wage work disappeared, Mary Snyder did not begin with outside capital: in 1932 she obtained a chauffeur’s license and turned her own car into both workplace and working capital, exposing the same asset to uncertain fares, commercial wear, and personal safety risk.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate.** Retrospective local reporting supports Snyder’s job loss, unsuccessful job search, ownership of the car, license, and 1932 taxi entry. It also supports her later partnership with Anne Jordan, their two-vehicle Mari-An Cab Company, and Snyder’s operating continuity. Startup finances, fares, profit, insurance, and exact partnership shares remain unknown.

## Why It Changes Understanding

The car makes women’s operating authority physical: Snyder controlled an asset, put it at risk, and personally delivered the service. Later durability depended on Jordan, licensing, customers, schedules, safety, and property—not solitary self-reliance.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

Preserving the car for private use or continuing to wait for wage employment was structurally safer. Snyder instead converted possession into licensed market entry before demand was proved. Builders should care because control begins with accepting responsibility for an asset’s productive use, not with freedom from dependencies.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

Mary Snyder made her own car carry two jobs at once: it was both her capital and her workplace.

## Mandatory Placement

State the own-car/licensed-entry truth on the opening page and within the first 10 percent, before longevity, mileage, or sale. Explicitly rewind from Barta’s August 1933 founding to Snyder’s 1932 entry.

## Omission Risk

**Automatic failure if missing or weakened.** Without the dual-use asset and direct exposure, the chapter becomes a generic celebration of women in business rather than an attributable market-entry decision.

## Exact Inheritance and Transition

Receive labor authority as a city condition, not a personal handoff. End on coordinated operating capability—asset, license, partner, routes, schedules, safety, customers, and property—then state that Chapter 22 rewinds again to the 1928 window project. No Snyder–Wood relationship is established.

## Prohibited Additions

Do not call Mari-An a Cedar Rapids company: it was Marion-based, though Snyder grew up in Cedar Rapids and served a connected regional market. Do not erase Anne Jordan from joint company or property commitments. Do not invent motive, dialogue, car value, debt, revenue, profit, ownership shares, scenes, or anticipated danger.
