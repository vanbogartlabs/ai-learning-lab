# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 27: Keith Barnes

## The One Unforgettable Truth

Barnes did not merely buy a reported approximately $16 million plant: Cedar Rapids Meats accepted its union contract and continuing commercial arrangements, kept production going for roughly six years, and still could not own the relationships that made the property productive.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate/qualified.** Repository synthesis of contemporary reporting identifies Barnes as head of the buyer, supports the reported transaction scale and inherited arrangements, and attributes the 1990 closure announcement to him. It does not establish his personal investment, guarantees, ownership share, or a sole cause of failure.

## Why It Changes Understanding

The six-year duration prevents instant-failure caricature; the collapse prevents rescue mythology. Barnes accepted responsibility for a living network, not just machinery and land, yet title could not command labor, credit, supply, customers, shipments, pensions, utilities, or markets.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

Acquiring selected assets, waiting for liquidation, or avoiding the distressed plant was structurally safer. Barnes instead pursued continuity under inherited dependence. Builders should remember that accepting system responsibility is consequential even when control remains partial and the outcome fails.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

Barnes bought the plant and kept it running for six years, but he could not buy the cooperation of every relationship it needed.
