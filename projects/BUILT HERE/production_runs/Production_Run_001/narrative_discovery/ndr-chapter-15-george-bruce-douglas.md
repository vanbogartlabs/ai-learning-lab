# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 15: George Bruce Douglas

## The One Unforgettable Truth

After catastrophic human loss destroyed the starch works, George Bruce Douglas participated in his family’s choice to preserve industrial possibility by selling the ruined site—and ending Douglas control.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate.** The works exploded on May 22, 1919; death counts and loss figures conflict. Repository summaries support a Douglas-family sale to Penick & Ford, variously dated late 1919 or February 1920. They support George’s participation, not sole authority, negotiation, culpability, motive, or transaction terms.

## Why It Changes Understanding

Douglas’s consequential act was relinquishment, not another expansion. Continuity could survive only by separating the site’s industrial future from the family name. The sale preserved possibility while doing nothing to restore lives, homes, wages, or trust; industrial retention and human obligation must remain distinct.

## Courageous Decision and Meaning

> Despite a ruined works, uncertain claims and value, civic scrutiny, and unresolved obligations, Douglas participated in the family sale to Penick & Ford so industrial use might continue without Douglas control.

Retaining the site, abandoning industrial use, or attempting a family-controlled rebuild were analytical alternatives. The chapter shows that persistence can require surrendering ownership rather than protecting identity.

## Mandatory Placement and Chronology

Open with or immediately announce the explicit reset: **the narrative returns from Chapter 14’s 1945–1946 flash-forward to May 22, 1919.** Place the sale-as-relinquishment truth within the first 10 percent; the catastrophe supplies moral pressure, not spectacle.

## Omission Risk

**Automatic failure if missing or weakened.** Without family-level attribution and ended control, the chapter becomes disaster chronicle, false redemption, or unsupported lone-hero narrative.

## Exact Inheritance

End with: **A damaged but connected industrial site, workforce, and unresolved obligations pass to Penick.** The handoff is transactional and structural.

## Prohibited Additions

Do not invent cause, dialogue, grief, remorse, liability, insurance, compensation, sale price, George’s sole authority, or guarantees of rebuilding or employment.
