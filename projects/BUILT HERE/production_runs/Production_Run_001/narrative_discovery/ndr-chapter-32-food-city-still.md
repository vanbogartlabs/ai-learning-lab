# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 32: Food City, Still

## The One Unforgettable Truth

In April 2020, ADM temporarily idled Cedar Rapids dry-mill ethanol production, redirected parts of its processing response toward other products, and announced about ninety local furloughs; Chris Cuddy, leader of the relevant business domain, publicly called the actions difficult without claiming personal approval.

## Why It Is Unforgettable

Adaptation and interruption were the same corporate act. ADM could redirect a portfolio among fuel, industrial alcohol or hand sanitizer, and containerboard starch; workers could not redirect household obligations on comparable terms. Cuddy makes accountability visible, but his title and quotation do not make the decisions his.

## Opening Image and Mandatory Placement

Open with one corn-processing system splitting under shock: the dry mill idled, other product pathways emphasized, and roughly ninety Cedar Rapids workers facing furlough. Keep ADM as the grammatical actor before introducing Cuddy. Place this contrast within the first 10 percent.

## Narrative Force and Governing Question

**Force:** industrial flexibility belongs to whoever controls allocation, while its consequences become local. **Governing question:** What does a city still possess when its plants remain useful but a global owner chooses their use?

## Evidence Boundary and Confidence

**Confidence: High for ADM’s announcements, Cuddy’s role and words, and the furlough count; qualified for routing and authority.** Plant-specific quantities, approval chains, restart dates, and individual worker outcomes remain unknown. Cuddy must not be portrayed as proposer, approver, signer, or final decider. Announced medical benefits and unemployment eligibility mitigated uncertainty; they do not erase it.
