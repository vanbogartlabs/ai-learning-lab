# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 8: Alois Blaha

## The One Truth

A November 1897 publication of the incorporation articles names Alois Bláha with Josef Žbánek and Hynek Breuer as the three signatories forming the Z.C.B.J. corporation and identifies Bláha as its initial secretary within a three-officer executive committee; later records place him at a 1899 convention and again identify him as national secretary in 1917, without proving uninterrupted tenure, sole control, a specific financial decision, or consequence.

## Why It Matters

A mutual-benefit institution can disappear into abstraction. The 1897 articles make its legal structure concrete: three signatories, a three-officer executive committee, an initial secretary, and a Cedar Rapids principal office. The unforgettable contradiction is that this establishes Bláha's bounded institutional role while preserving co-agency and proving no sole founding, individual reserve or claim control, personal guarantee, accepted downside, or consequence.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning
