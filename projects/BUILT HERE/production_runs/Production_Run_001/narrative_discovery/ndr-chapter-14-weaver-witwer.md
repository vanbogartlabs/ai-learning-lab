## The One Unforgettable Truth

In 1945, before the postwar market was settled, Witwer committed his company to a reported $500,000 facility designed to make processing, packaging, storage, rail, trucking, wholesaling, and retailer service operate as one system.

## Evidence and Confidence

## Why It Changes Understanding

Witwer was not merely building a larger warehouse. He was concentrating many uncertain dependencies so heterogeneous goods could become dependable availability for retailers. The same integration that increased coordination also multiplied the ways one connected system could fail.

## Courageous Decision and Meaning

> Despite uncertain postwar demand, product mix, retailer acceptance, credit, transport balance, spoilage, and operating coordination, Witwer committed to an integrated distribution plant rather than expand incrementally or remain a conventional wholesaler.

The safer course was continued use of converted premises or staged expansion. Builders should care because customer usefulness often requires committing to the whole middle system before demand proves it.
