# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 13: Robert Stuart

## The One Unforgettable Truth

After rebuilding a larger oat mill following the 1887 fire, Stuart accepted broader organization and reduced local control so enlarged production could become a recognizable promise beyond Cedar Rapids.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate.** Repository summaries associate Stuart with continuation after repeated fire, a larger mill open by May 1888, and entry into the multi-city American Cereal combination. Founding credit is shared, the first fire is dated 1874 or 1876, and 1888/1891 may mark different combination steps. The record supports recommitment followed by combination, not sole founding, brand invention, or a recovered motive.

## Why It Changes Understanding

Stuart’s defining problem was not simply rebuilding. The larger mill intensified the unanswered question of demand. His consequential choice was to let a local enterprise depend on partners and systems beyond his control so a commodity could travel as a repeatable market promise.

## Courageous Decision and Meaning

> Despite repeated fire, enlarged capacity without assured demand, and reduced local control, Stuart chose the broader American Cereal combination because scale required market reach and organization beyond an independent mill.

Waiting, rebuilding only prior capacity, or remaining independent was structurally safer. Builders today inherit the same choice: capability may become useful only through dependence on systems they do not command.
