# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 1: Nicholas B. Brown

## The One Truth

Brown is reported to have participated in the collective 1841 effort that gave Rapids City **805 planned lots** across slightly more than sixty blocks before the repository demonstrates proven lot demand or dependable river power. The plan made uncertainty concrete enough for others to enter; it did not make the proposition secure.

## Why It Changes Understanding

Brown matters not as a solitary founder of an inevitable city, but as a participant who helped create commercial form before success was visible. The 805 lots turn “organization before proof” into a physical business decision: inventory preceded its demonstrated market and economic engine.

## Courageous Decision

> Despite unresolved control, demand, participant roles, and productive power, Brown chose to help organize a large surveyed town proposition because his participation indicates that he treated a defined opportunity as worth creating before success was proven.

The “because” clause interprets reported conduct; it is not Brown’s recorded belief.

## Exact Ten-Year Reader-Retell Sentence

> **Nicholas B. Brown helped plan 805 lots before Rapids City had proven buyers or dependable power.**

## Mandatory Chapter Placement

Place this truth on the opening page and within the first 10 percent, before land chronology or participant detail. Pair 805 lots immediately with the absence of demonstrated demand and dependable power, and identify Brown’s role as collective and reported.

## Evidence Boundary

Repository summaries associate Brown with the collective 1841 survey/planning effort. A professional historic-context study, as summarized in the repository, reports slightly more than sixty blocks and 805 planned lots. No original plat, agreement, deed chain, or Brown statement was inspected for this run. Participant lists and capacities differ. The survey established describable inventory, not clean title, sales, municipal success, or dependable power.

## What Must Never Be Lost

- The exact scale: 805 planned lots.
- Inventory preceded demonstrated buyers and dependable power.
- Brown’s role was collective, reported, and bounded.
- Form made the proposition enterable, not secure.

## What Must Never Be Added

- Sole-founder, sole-owner, sole-surveyor, or sole-designer credit.
- Proven sales, clean title, exact investment, return, or authority.
- Invented motive, certainty, emotion, dialogue, or private vision.
- A claim that the survey created the later city or conquered the river.
