# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 25: Arthur Collins

## The One Unforgettable Truth

In 1971, working capital came with governing power: Arthur Collins allowed a reported $35 million North American Rockwell convertible-stock investment and debt restructuring to proceed while Rockwell gained the right to elect a board majority—preserving operating possibility by surrendering effective founder control.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate/qualified.** A detailed later synthesis reports the May agreement, $35 million instrument, majority-board rights, debt restructuring, August 31 shareholder action, September 2 investment and election of seven Rockwell directors, Collins’s November removal, and December 7 resignation. Annual reports, agreements, minutes, bank records, proxy materials, and securities filings remain publication-critical verification gaps.

## Why It Changes Understanding

The transaction makes dependence concrete. Capital was not neutral cash; it carried an allocation of authority. Collins’s technical achievement and the company’s continuity did not guarantee that the founder could finance or govern the institution bearing his name.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

Earlier spending cuts, narrower products, asset sales, different financing, another investor, merger, or court restructuring are analytical alternatives; the record does not establish that all remained available or safe. Collins accepted governance exposure amid working-capital pressure. Builders should care because survival, ownership, and control can become separate outcomes.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

Arthur Collins kept the company operating by accepting money that could outvote him.

## Mandatory Placement

Place the qualified 1971 capital-for-board-rights truth on the opening page and within the first 10 percent, before Apollo or C-System spectacle. Distinguish May agreement, August shareholder action, September closing and board election, November removal, December resignation, and the later 1973 merger.

## Omission Risk

**Automatic failure if missing or weakened.** Without the convertible-stock instrument and majority-board right, the chapter repeats wartime scale or turns loss of control into vague tragedy.
