# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 19: Paul Lattner

## The One Truth

In 1909 Paul and Joe Lattner commissioned purpose-built premises for Cedar Rapids Auto Company before the surviving record can establish demand sufficient to support them—putting fixed space behind a machine whose fuel or charging, parts, repairs, mechanics, roads, finance, and resale ecology was still incomplete.

## Why It Matters

The building makes Lattner’s market-making visible. He was not simply selling automobiles; the fixed premises made the brothers answerable for what happened after novelty became ownership. Paul’s later repair-expense pitch and electric-car offer show that operating cost and propulsion were still unsettled.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

Paul, acting with Joe, committed dedicated space rather than using temporary or adapted premises or waiting for demand and support systems to mature. The decision accepted building or occupancy, inventory, staffing, service, and reputational exposure; title, cost, financing, lease terms, guarantees, and exact division of authority remain unknown.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

The Lattners built a home for the automobile trade before Cedar Rapids had finished building everything an automobile needed after the sale.

## Mandatory Placement

Open by explicitly rewinding from Chapter 18’s 1926 relocation and later consequences to the Lattners’ 1909 premises commitment. Put the dedicated-building-before-ecosystem truth on the opening page and within the first 10 percent.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate/qualified.** Repository summaries support the joint 1909 commission at 212–216 Fourth Avenue, Paul’s later repair-economics promotion, Automobile Row occupancy, and 1919 Milburn Light Electric advertisement. Demand, sales, project cost, ownership, financing, customer savings, and the contested 1902 “first dealership” claim are unresolved.

## Omission Risk

Without this truth, the chapter becomes automobile nostalgia or district history. It loses the fixed commitment that distinguishes Paul and Joe’s risk and the insight that a durable-product market depends on support after sale.

## Frozen Inheritance and Transition

Receive Chapter 18 through an explicit chronology rewind: machinery capability has created roads and service ecosystems, but the narrative returns to 1909 to show automobile-market formation. Leave Chapter 20: **Mobility infrastructure enlarges industry and employment while increasing the need for worker counterpower.** No personal handoff to Milo Barta is implied.

## What Must Never Be Added

No sole credit to Paul, unqualified “first” claim, invented motive or dialogue, exact finances or sales, verified repair savings, control over the full support ecology, inevitable gasoline dominance, or false linear handoff from Ronk.
