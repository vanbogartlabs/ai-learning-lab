# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 24: Arthur Collins

## The One Unforgettable Truth

In 1942 Collins Radio enlarged its Thirty-Fifth Street plant and occupied leased locations around Cedar Rapids, gaining wartime capacity by scattering work across more space, people, and systems while procurement authorities could shape requirements, materials, prices, labor matters, inspection, and termination.

## Evidence and Confidence

**Confidence: Moderate to high at Research stage.** Repository synthesis supports the 1942 additions and leased locations; the University of Iowa archive inventory identifies building-addition files, a plant survey, organization charts, salary and labor materials, price-adjustment matters, spare-parts files, and mobilization schedules. Exact authorizations, sites, leases, costs, contracts, workforce totals, and Collins’s personal cash exposure remain unknown.

## Why It Changes Understanding

Wartime growth was not merely more orders. Every added location increased capability and coordination burden while the dominant customer regime constrained the organization. The larger Collins became, the more its performance belonged to workers, managers, suppliers, landlords, inspectors, and public agencies—not Arthur alone.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

Accepting less work, subcontracting more, expanding less, or remaining concentrated was structurally safer. Collins accepted founder-level fixed-capacity, technical, contractual, and reputational exposure so a young organization could reproduce quality through many people and places. Builders should care because scale increases both capability and dependence.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

Arthur Collins scaled radio production by making one young company reproduce technical quality across many places, people, and customer-controlled rules.

## Mandatory Placement

Place the 1942 added-and-leased-capacity truth on the opening page and within the first 10 percent, before product or workforce spectacle. Begin with the capacity commitment, not childhood radio or company formation.

## Omission Risk

**Automatic failure if missing or weakened.** Without distributed space plus customer power, the chapter becomes a celebratory wartime montage or repeats Chapter 23’s organization story.

## Exact Inheritance and Transition

Receive Chapter 23’s durable young organization without replaying formation. End with: **Distributed technical capacity—facilities, workforce, administration, quality routines, and customer coordination—that must survive the wartime demand regime.** Chapter 25 receives that institution and asks about the specific 1971 financing and governance rights.

Exclude Apollo, C-System debt, Rockwell investment, and 1971 control loss from this chapter’s discovery.
