# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 2: George Greene

## The One Truth

George Greene’s importance is his reported 1841 entry into Rapids City while the town’s demand and river-powered economic engine remained unproven: his participation made a defined proposition a shared commitment. The surviving record cannot recover exactly what interest he acquired, but that modern absence does not establish what Greene did or did not know.

## Why It Changes Understanding

Greene matters not because he solved Rapids City’s uncertainties, but because he accepted a place inside them. Brown’s survey had made the opportunity legible; Greene’s entry showed another participant was willing to share its exposure before buyers or dependable power made it secure.

## Courageous Decision

> Despite unproven demand, unresolved participant roles, and no dependable river-powered engine, Greene reportedly chose to accept an interest in Rapids City because his participation indicates that he treated the defined but unsecured proposition as worth joining.

## Ten-Year Reader-Retell Sentence

> George Greene reportedly entered Rapids City before its demand or economic engine was proven, turning a surveyed proposition into shared commitment even though the surviving record cannot tell us exactly what interest he acquired.

## Placement

State the reported entry and its source limitation on the opening page; establish shared commitment within the first 10 percent. Qualify the missing transaction once, then keep the story on Greene’s decision rather than archival absence.

## Evidence Boundary

Supported: Greene is repeatedly associated with the 1841 venture; a later account reports that he accepted or purchased an interest; Rapids City had 805 planned lots; dependable demand and river power were not established. The exact asset, terms, price, fraction, legal role, knowledge, and motive remain unknown. “Shared commitment” is a bounded interpretation of participation, not sole causation.

## What Must Never Be Lost

- Greene’s reported entry into another person’s still-unproven proposition.
- The distinction between uncertainty in the venture and gaps in the modern record.
- The consequence: broader participation increased pressure to create productive value.

## What Must Never Be Added

- Any claim that missing documentation proves Greene knew nothing, acted blindly, or ignored known defects.
- Exact transaction terms, motives, thoughts, dialogue, or personal financial exposure.
- Sole-founder, sole-purchaser, title-curer, surveyor, financier, or later-railroad heroics.
