# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 5: George Greene

## The One Truth

After river transport exposed its limits, George Greene participated in promoting fixed rail connection before route, capital, construction, traffic, and dependable operation were secure. Iron track did not end dependence; it reorganized dependence around collective capital, infrastructure, labor, schedules, rates, maintenance, and corporate operators beyond one promoter's control.

## Why It Matters

This is Greene's distinct rail decision, not Chapter 2's land entry. It makes his courage neither technological prophecy nor solitary authorship, but renewed participation in an uncertain connection system. Another builder should remember that a more dependable platform can still require commitment before its network works—and can exchange one constraint for another.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

> Despite river transport's practical limits and an unproved rail network, Greene chose to participate in promoting fixed connection because his repeated involvement supports the inference that organized external circulation was worth pursuing before the system was secure.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

> Greene helped promote rail after the river showed its limits, knowing in practice that fixed iron reorganized dependence rather than eliminating it.

## Mandatory Placement

State the One Truth on the opening page and within the first 10 percent, before rail-arrival or later facility detail. Otherwise consequence will eclipse decision and Greene may be miscast as the railroad's sole builder.

## Evidence and Confidence

Repository summaries associate Greene with railroad promotion, report river freight's dependence on favorable water, and place the line's arrival in June 1859. **Confidence: Moderate and qualified.** Greene's exact title, subscription, authority, negotiations, loss, and role in the Fourth Street offer are unknown.

## Omission Risk

Without this truth, Chapter 5 either repeats Greene's Chapter 2 land entry or becomes generic rail history. It would lose the choice to pursue a collective system before proof and the contradiction between improved dependability and new network dependence.

## Frozen Inheritance and Transition

Receive Chapter 4's enlarged two-bank city and unresolved need for dependable external connection. Leave Chapter 6 **rail-enabled circulation**: credible regional movement of people, inputs, goods, and information, without claiming Greene financed Young, selected his addition, controlled streetcars, or caused sales.

## What Must Never Be Added

No invented motive, dialogue, scene, quantified personal exposure, sole-builder credit, guaranteed success, or causal claim that Greene created later industrial growth.
