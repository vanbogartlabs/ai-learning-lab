# Narrative Discovery Record — Chapter 9: T. M. Sinclair

## The One Truth

After a reported 15,039 hogs in one season of leased temporary quarters, T. M. Sinclair committed the enterprise to sixteen permanent acres beside two rail lines. The first figure proved throughput, not profit or durability; the larger site made receiving, processing, preservation, packaging, repair, by-products, labor, and shipment one concentrated system before that system was secure.

## Why It Matters

The swift move from an icehouse test to fixed rail-integrated scale makes Sinclair's decision unforgettable. Integration increased operating capability while multiplying dependence and concentrating fire, machinery, perishability, waste, and neighborhood burden. Another builder should care because partial proof can justify commitment without resolving whether an enlarged system will remain coordinated or whom its failures will expose.

## Entrepreneurial Meaning

> Despite only temporary proof and uncertain long-term demand, Sinclair chose a permanent sixteen-acre rail-linked site because his actions support the inference that coordinated processing and shipment could turn initial throughput into durable industrial capability.

## Reader-Retell Sentence

> One reported season in a leased icehouse led Sinclair to commit to sixteen rail-linked acres before the integrated system's durability was proved.

## Mandatory Placement

Place the icehouse-to-sixteen-acres reversal on the opening page and within the first 10 percent. State that 15,039 indicates throughput, not profit, before any scale or success language.

## Evidence and Confidence

Repository summaries report the 1871 icehouse lease, 15,039 first-season hogs, the February 1872 sixteen-acre acquisition beside two rail lines, and track entering the plant. **Confidence: Moderate and qualified; Low to Moderate for track terms and authority.** Capital, profit, construction terms, and Sinclair's exact exposure remain unknown.

## Omission Risk

Without this truth, the chapter becomes packing-house chronology or founder triumph. It loses the decision between temporary proof and permanent concentration, as well as the system's inseparable productive and human burdens.

## Frozen Inheritance and Transition

Explicitly rewind to 1871 from Chapters 7–8; do not imply a personal handoff. Leave Chapter 10 an operating company, specialized workforce, rail-served plant, concentrated fortune, public burdens, and unresolved succession and stewardship after Sinclair's death—not Caroline's unrestricted corporate control.

## What Must Never Be Added
