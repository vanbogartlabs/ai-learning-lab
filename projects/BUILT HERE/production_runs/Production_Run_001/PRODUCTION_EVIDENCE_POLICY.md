# Production Run 001 — Production Evidence Policy

**Status:** Approved by Project Owner on 2026-07-19

## Production Authorization

Production Run 001 may draft every frozen chapter using the strongest currently available evidence. Completion of manuscript prose does not represent completion of historical research.

## Mandatory Controls

1. Never invent facts.
2. Never invent motives.
3. Never invent dialogue.
4. Qualify uncertainty wherever the evidence does not support a stronger statement.
5. Mark every material claim that would benefit from stronger evidence in `EVIDENCE_IMPROVEMENT_QUEUE.md` and its chapter claim map.
6. Maintain Evidence Improvement coverage for all 35 chapters.
7. Preserve co-agency and distributed authority whenever the evidence does not support exclusive person-level credit.
8. Do not convert title, association, public quotation, corporate action, cost, or later success into unsupported personal authority, risk, causation, or consequence.

## Stop Condition

Only a chapter-specific evidence failure that prevents responsible narration may stop that chapter. Isolated evidence weakness does not stop other production lanes or manuscript completion.

Responsible narration may:

- report the strongest supported action;
- identify whether agency is individual, shared, institutional, or publicly representative;
- distinguish documented action from analytical interpretation;
- state what current evidence does not establish;
- preserve unresolved consequence, funding, authority, motive, or outcome questions for later expansion.

Responsible narration may not manufacture a courageous decision merely to satisfy a narrative template. When a decision's authority or personal risk remains uncertain, the chapter must say so and center only the strongest supportable action.

## Post-Manuscript Evidence Expansion Pass

After a complete 35-chapter manuscript exists, execute a dedicated Evidence Expansion Pass to:

- strengthen citations;
- locate additional primary sources;
- resolve outstanding evidence questions;
- replace qualified statements with stronger supported statements when warranted;
- improve, not redesign or rewrite, the manuscript.

The Evidence Expansion Pass must preserve valid prose and revise only claims whose evidentiary status materially improves.
