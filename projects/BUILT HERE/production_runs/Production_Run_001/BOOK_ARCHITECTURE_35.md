## Authority and Use

This artifact preserves the approved 35-slot numbering and records the result of book-wide architecture validation. It supersedes the five-chapter production scope in `BATCH_ARCHITECTURE.md`, but it does not approve blocked chapters for production.

Historical Version 1/1.5 chapter files were used only to identify candidate protagonists, decisions, transitions, and evidence gaps. They do not control Version 2 prose or factual claims.

No chapter may be drafted from this map alone. Every slot requires a validated person-level protagonist, a single defining courageous decision, a specific inherited condition, and the required Research, Character Brief, NDR, and Intent artifacts.

## Status Key

- **PASS:** The slot has a person-centered, distinct, necessary architectural function. Evidence must still pass normal upstream validation.
- **BLOCKED:** The slot lacks verified protagonist agency, duplicates another chapter, has an unresolved transition, or cannot yet satisfy the Courage Principles.
- **BRAID:** The approved sequence intentionally rewinds or overlaps chronology. The temporal reset must be explicit in upstream artifacts and eventual prose.

## Six-Movement Book Arc

1. **Chapters 1–6 — Make possibility usable:** plat → participation → power → local crossing → external rail → outward market.
2. **Chapters 7–12 — Make entry durable:** neighborhood commerce → mutual aid → industrial system → wealth obligation → household security → scheduled movement.
3. **Chapters 13–22 — Make scale consequential:** brand → distribution → catastrophe → rebuilding → machinery → mobility → labor authority → women's authority → collaborative production.
4. **Chapters 23–28 — Confront dependence and control:** Depression formation → wartime customer power → founder-control tradeoff → suburban market → failed plant continuity → access under exclusion.
5. **Chapters 29–34 — Preserve choice through disruption:** rescue mission → rebuild capability → incubator creation → industrial adaptation → retained technical skill → disaster-era survival.
6. **Chapter 35 — Accept responsibility for the next opening:** inherited opportunity becomes an authored commitment and a duty to leave something usable.

## Book-Wide Courage Arc Assessment

The intended courage arc is coherent in outline but **fails validation at present** because 16 chapter slots do not yet establish a person with a distinct, consequential decision and responsibility for outcome.

The blocked slots create two structural troughs:

1. **Chapters 7–12:** community, mutual aid, wealth, housing, and transit are thematically necessary, but person-level agency is insufficiently established.
2. **Chapters 29–35:** the ending engine has the correct themes but currently relies on repeated protagonists, public spokespeople, broad civic topics, or objectives rather than increasingly consequential decisions.

## Transition Controls

1. Every chapter must end with a specific usable condition or unresolved obligation, not a generic lesson.
2. The next chapter may inherit a city condition without implying undocumented personal contact.
3. Every approved chronology braid must be signposted as a reset or parallel history.
4. Ch5 must pass rail-enabled circulation to Young in Ch6.
5. Ch7→8→9 must be an explicit immigrant/community/industrial braid, not false linear chronology.
6. Ch14→15, Ch18→19, Ch27→28, Ch33→34, and Ch34→35 require explicit resets.
7. Ch30 must hand the recovered factory/market platform to the actual Ch31 founder; the Ann Poe/Pinkerton mismatch cannot remain.
8. Repeated protagonists pass only if the later decision changes uncertainty, responsibility, and inheritance.
9. No handoff may assert sole causation, inevitability, private motive, or an unsupported relationship.

## Final Chapter Contract

Chapter 35 remains a viable destination but **fails its current evidence gate**.

The eventual final chapter must:

1. **Receive:** place one current builder inside an opening created by prior recovery and institutional design.
2. **Choose:** show a documented larger obligation that inherited structures cannot decide for the builder.
3. **Reinterpret:** activate only the callbacks supported by that decision—Brown's lines, King's crossing, temporary forms, damaged buildings, or NewBo's bounded entry.
4. **Assume responsibility:** establish inheritance as an obligation to preserve or create a usable next step.
5. **Hand off:** transfer the final movement to an unnamed future builder without predicting success.

If Sydney Rieckhoff's person-level evidence cannot support those five functions, she must remain a bounded illustrative echo rather than the protagonist of a manufactured culmination.
