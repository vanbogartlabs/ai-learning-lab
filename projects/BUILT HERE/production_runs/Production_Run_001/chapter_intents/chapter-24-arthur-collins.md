# Chapter Intent — Chapter 24: The Arsenal of Skills

## Chapter Title

**The Arsenal of Skills**

## Entrepreneur

**Arthur A. Collins** — use the approved Character Brief at `../character_briefs/arthur-collins.md`. Collins remains the founder-level protagonist, but workers, managers, landlords, suppliers, inspectors, procurement officials, and public agencies retain independent roles. This chapter covers only the 1942 capacity commitment.

## One-Sentence Chapter Promise

> This chapter is the story of Arthur Collins committing a young radio company to wartime capacity across added plant sections and leased Cedar Rapids locations, gaining technical reach while multiplying dependence on people, systems, and customer-controlled rules.

## Why This Chapter Exists

Chapter 23 asks whether personal skill can become an entity. This chapter asks whether that entity can reproduce technical quality through many people and places under a powerful wartime customer regime. Growth is not a montage of orders or workers; it is a capacity decision that expands fixed obligations, coordination burden, and dependence at the same time.

This is the second distinct Collins decision. It must not replay the several-stage company formation, and it must stop before postwar capital and governance become the central conflict. The chapter advances the book’s dependence-and-control movement by showing customer-shaped scale: capability increases while independent discretion narrows.

## Central Business Problem

How can Collins enlarge a young technical organization fast enough to perform wartime communications work when added space, leased locations, workers, materials, specifications, prices, inspections, labor rules, and contract termination must all be coordinated under procurement authority the company does not control?

The problem is not simply producing more units. The company must make quality repeatable across dispersed capacity while accepting overhead and systems that wartime demand may not sustain.

## Defining Decision

In 1942 Collins chooses to enlarge fixed and leased capacity so Collins Radio can perform wartime communications work at organizational scale. The company adds sections to the Thirty-Fifth Street plant and occupies leased locations around Cedar Rapids rather than limiting work, subcontracting more, expanding less, or remaining concentrated.

This is a capacity commitment under customer power. It is not a second company founding, generic “choice to grow,” product breakthrough, or prelude that may be replaced by the later Rockwell control bargain.

## Stakes

- **For Collins:** Founder-level responsibility extends to fixed capacity, distributed operations, technical quality, hiring, training, coordination, contract performance, and reputational exposure.
- **For workers and households:** Recruitment, classification, training, pay, hours, safety, and continued employment depend on orders and operating systems; no individual experience may be inferred from archive labels.
- **For managers, suppliers, landlords, and inspectors:** Each carries distinct responsibility for making scattered capacity function.
- **For the company:** Wartime demand can justify additions while scarce materials, price rules, inspection, cancellation, termination, and postwar overhead remain outside its sole control.
- **For public customers:** Communications equipment must satisfy requirements through a larger organization whose execution no single founder can personally perform.

## Emotional Arc

**Beginning — Capacity before spectacle:** Begin with the 1942 additions and leased Cedar Rapids locations. Put the added-and-leased-capacity truth on the opening page and within the first ten percent, before products, contracts, or workforce spectacle.

**Middle — Every new space adds a system:** Plant surveys, organization charts, salary and labor files, spare-parts records, price adjustments, and mobilization schedules make scale visible as coordination burden, not as triumphal totals.

**Decision — The young entity becomes distributed:** Collins accepts more fixed and leased capacity under procurement rules that can shape requirements and end demand.

**End — Capability becomes dependence:** The organization can now reproduce technical work across people and places, but the customer regime that forced the capacity may not preserve it. The emotional force lies in obligation becoming dependence, not invented excitement, sacrifice, fear, or patriotic certainty.

## Reader Takeaway

> Arthur Collins scaled radio production by making one young company reproduce technical quality across many places, people, and customer-controlled rules.

Scale increases both capability and the number of authorities on which performance depends.

## Connection to Cedar Rapids

Cedar Rapids gave Collins a recently built plant, additional leasable industrial and commercial space, utilities, transport, labor, suppliers, and a city accustomed to serving distant markets. Distributed local space enabled expansion before one campus could contain the work.

Collins Radio gave Cedar Rapids expanded technical and administrative work and routines capable of coordinating specifications, quality, labor, records, and multiple locations. The contribution was collective; the present evidence does not establish which individuals obtained which jobs, wages, injuries, promotions, or lasting careers.
