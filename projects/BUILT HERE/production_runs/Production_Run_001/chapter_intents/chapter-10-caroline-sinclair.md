## Central Business Problem

How does a client carry a substantial property commission from purchase through design succession and construction to occupancy when one architect leaves—and what can that completed execution prove when funding authority and personal downside remain unknown?

The documented project turn is Allardt's departure and Caroline's hiring of Josselyn & Taylor. The record supports continuation through replacement; it does not establish delay, conflict, redesign cost, overrun, financing pressure, or Caroline's private response.

## Defining Decision

The evidence does not support a courageous capital-allocation decision or a verified personal-risk claim. The chapter must center the strongest supportable action:

> Caroline purchased the property in 1884, commissioned Maximillian Allardt, hired Josselyn & Taylor after Allardt left the project, oversaw construction, and brought the reported $55,000 residence to completion and occupancy.

The reported total establishes project scale, not source or legal control of funds, debt, sacrifice, loss, or accepted downside.
