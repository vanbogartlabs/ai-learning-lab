# Chapter Intent — Chapter 23: Building Through the Depression

## Chapter Title

**Building Through the Depression**

## Entrepreneur

**Arthur A. Collins** — use the approved Character Brief at `../character_briefs/arthur-collins.md`. This chapter covers only Collins’s 1931–1937 formation decision. Wartime capacity belongs to Chapter 24; postwar financing and loss of control belong to Chapter 25.

## One-Sentence Chapter Promise

> This chapter is the story of Arthur Collins moving specialized radio work through successive commercial and legal thresholds during the Depression until personal capability became an operating company with obligations.

## Why This Chapter Exists

This chapter begins the book’s movement on dependence and control at its smallest consequential threshold: a customer cannot enlarge a technical organization until that organization exists. It rejects the clean founding myth in favor of the ordinary work that makes skill durable—selling, collecting, bidding, employing, recording, naming, chartering, and accepting obligations.

Chapter 23 is the first of three distinct Collins decisions. It creates the entity. Chapter 24 will enlarge that entity through many people and locations under wartime customer power. Chapter 25 will confront a specific capital-and-governance bargain. Neither later decision may be used to make formation look inevitable or more dramatic.

## Central Business Problem

How can Collins convert technical ability into a company whose commercial and legal routines can repeat beyond his own hands while customers, cash, staffing, and survival remain uncertain during economic contraction?

A working radio does not create an operating enterprise. The company must sell, collect payment, bid, keep records, employ people, answer to customers, and assume legal obligations repeatedly.

## Defining Decision

Collins chooses during 1931–1937 to move specialized radio work through successive organizational commitments rather than preserve it as bounded amateur, consulting, or individual practice. The supported thresholds are Arthur Collins Radio Laboratories in 1931, use of the Collins Radio name by late 1932, a Delaware charter in 1933, and Iowa reincorporation in 1937.

The decision is formation through a sequence, not one ceremonial founding date. The chapter must define each threshold rather than collapsing them or selecting one as uncontested.

## Stakes

- **For Collins:** His professional name and standing become answerable for customers, bids, receipts, employees, records, and entity-level obligations.
- **For early customers:** Technical performance must be joined by delivery, service, and a business capable of remaining accountable.
- **For employees and collaborators:** Their work depends on a young firm converting orders and receipts into repeatable operation.
- **For the company:** Economic contraction can expose weak collection, capitalization, customer demand, and administrative routines before the organization is secure.
- **Evidence limit:** Exact ownership, capitalization, loans, guarantees, collateral, payroll, margins, household contribution, profit, and losses are unknown and may not be supplied as stakes.

## Emotional Arc

**Beginning — No single birthday:** Shift explicitly from Wood’s 1928 window project to Collins’s 1931–1937 formation. Place the several-threshold truth on the opening page and within the first ten percent; do not open with childhood radio, Byrd spectacle, wartime production, or aerospace reach.

**Middle — Skill acquires obligations:** Receipts, bids, attorneys, customers, employees, and legal forms reveal that the less glamorous routines are the mechanism by which technical work becomes durable.

**Decision — Professional name becomes institutional responsibility:** Collins repeatedly chooses greater formal commitment instead of keeping work within personal capacity.

**End — A company exists, but remains young:** The reader moves from recognizable technical skill to a formal organization approaching dedicated capacity without knowing whether later scale will preserve reliability. The emotional force belongs in possibility becoming obligation, not invented confidence, hardship, or triumph.

## Reader Takeaway

> Arthur Collins founded a company more than once because each new legal and commercial threshold made his radio skill less personal and more durable.

Technical capability becomes inheritable when routines and obligations can outlast one person’s hands.

## Historical Material Required

1. **Opening-page truth:** Collins Radio had no single clean founding moment; distinguish laboratory activity in 1931, use of the Collins Radio name by late 1932, Delaware chartering in 1933, and Iowa reincorporation in 1937.
2. **Prior capability, tightly bounded:** Collins was a licensed amateur radio operator and had recognized technical ability before formation; use only enough to establish what had not yet become a company.
3. **Commercial routines:** Early receipt, cash-drawer, advertising, attorney, employment, customer, bid, government-work, and equipment record classes make organization tangible without treating folder titles as transaction proof.
4. **Depression pressure:** Establish economic contraction and uncertain customers and cash without claiming poverty, denied credit, or heroic solitude.
5. **Byrd work, if used:** Keep it a bounded distant-customer example whose contract terms and causal importance remain unverified; it cannot explain survival.
6. **Family context, if indispensable:** Merle Collins’s mortgage and farm-company failures may supply surrounding uncertainty but cannot be assigned as Arthur’s debt or personal loss.
7. **Closing threshold:** A dedicated Thirty-Fifth Street plant around 1940–1941 may appear only as the approaching capacity condition for Chapter 24.
8. **Evidence readiness:** Research, Character Brief, and NDR are PASS/approved for intent use at moderate/qualified confidence. Original filings and transaction records remain publication-level verification needs; no unverified financial specifics may enter prose.

## Historical Material To Avoid

- A single uncontested founding date, genius-in-a-workshop origin story, or incorporation treated as ceremonial paperwork after the “real” invention.
- General radio history, childhood-prodigy biography, product catalogs, or expedition spectacle that displaces company formation.
- Wartime additions, leased locations, wartime workforce or procurement systems; these belong to Chapter 24.
- Apollo, C-System development and debt, Rockwell financing, board rights, removal, resignation, or loss of control; these belong to Chapter 25.
- Claims that the Byrd work guaranteed survival, produced a known profit, or directly caused later contracts.
- Claims that Merle Collins’s failures became Arthur’s liabilities or that a Cedar Rapids bank financed, refused, or saved the company.
- Exact capitalization, ownership, loans, collateral, guarantees, revenue, profit, payroll, headcount, household contribution, customer results, or losses.
- Invented motive, emotion, dialogue, family conversation, workshop scene, customer exchange, or response to his father’s failures.
- Solitary-creator language that erases employees, customers, advisers, family context, suppliers, or institutions when records identify them.

## Connection to Cedar Rapids

Cedar Rapids gave Collins a home base, amateur-radio and family context, customers and professional relationships, legal and commercial institutions, utilities, transportation, industrial skills, labor, and space. It also supplied a demanding Depression environment in which no specific financing outcome may be assumed.

Collins gave Cedar Rapids a formal radio enterprise whose records, customer work, roles, and later fixed base broadened the city’s production vocabulary beyond food and machinery. At this stage, his contribution is the operating organization—not wartime totals, Apollo, or aerospace legacy.

## Transition

Receive Wood’s method—specification, other people’s work, commercial relationships, and accountable execution—as a city condition, never as a personal handoff or influence.

Close with the exact inheritance: **a durable but still young technical organization with formal identity, early customers, commercial routines, records, roles, a local base, and a dedicated-capacity threshold approaching.** Chapter 24 receives that organization in 1942 and asks whether it can reproduce technical quality through many people and places under customer-shaped procurement rules. Do not preview postwar finance or 1971 control.

## Chapter North Star

> If every paragraph disappeared except one idea, it would be: **Collins made technical skill durable by making his name answerable for an organization, not by having one more invention.**
