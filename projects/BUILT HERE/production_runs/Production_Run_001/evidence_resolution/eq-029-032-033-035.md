### Findings

- The two June 14 statements are the strongest newly inspected contemporaneous records. Both are expressly presented as statements from Naughton while the museum remained inaccessible and the extent of damage was unknown.

- NPR attributes artifact-cleaning work to curator Stefanie Kohn and volunteers, not to an emergency decision by Naughton. KWWL supplies damage context only. KCRG concerns citywide business recovery. The 2009 public-input page concerns rebuilding. None resolves emergency authority.
- The NCSML news index preserves additional contemporaneous article identifiers, but the inspected items and surviving text did not produce board minutes, emergency logs, staff directives, insurer/FEMA authorizations, or an interview allocating a discrete emergency decision to Naughton.
