## EQ-007 — Anna Jacobs / New Bohemia

### Gate tested

An inspectable record must attribute ownership, liability, operating authority, or another consequential decision to Anna Jacobs while preserving Anna Kurik's joint role. Collective operation or a jointly named lease statement does not by itself establish Jacobs's individual authority, liability, exposure, or consequence.

### Inspectable sources and identifiers

1. **Marlys A. Svendsen, National Register of Historic Places Registration Form, Bohemian Commercial Historic District, Linn County, Iowa (July 2002)** — complete 58-page PDF inspected from the City of Cedar Rapids historic-survey repository.
   - PDF: https://cms8.revize.com/revize/cedarrapids/Community%20Development/Planning/Historic%20Surveys%20and%20Data/nrhp/bcnhd.pdf
   - Relevant locations: physical PDF pages 11–12 and 21; continuation Section 8, page 17; bibliography on physical PDF page 30.
2. **Library of Congress item `17020640`, *Souvenir program of the fiftieth anniversary of the incorporation of the city of Cedar Rapids, Iowa*, 1906** — complete 70-image OCR derivative searched for Jacobs, Kurik, Blaha, Sinclair, Bohemian, and association terms.
   - Item: https://www.loc.gov/item/17020640/
   - Full OCR: https://tile.loc.gov/storage-services/public/gdcmassbookdig/souvenirprogramo00ceda/souvenirprogramo00ceda.text.txt

### Findings from inspected evidence

- The nomination says the Jacobs Building at 1121 Third Street SE was constructed in 1914, replacing two frame dwellings, and had two first-floor shop spaces and four flats above. It calls the property the “Jacobs Building,” but that label is not a deed, tax assessment, construction contract, or finding that Anna Jacobs held title.
- The nomination's significance narrative states: “Anna Jacobs and Anna Kurik operated Jacobs & Kurik Drygoods store and, for a time, leased space to a third ‘Anna,’ Anna Lesinger who worked as an undertaker from this address.” This is stronger inspectable support for shared operation and a leasing relationship than the original Research record had.
- The same sentence attributes operation and leasing to Jacobs and Kurik collectively. It does not identify which woman held title, signed a lease, owned inventory, supplied capital, controlled accounts, assumed partnership liability, set terms, or bore a loss or closure consequence. It therefore cannot be rewritten as an individual act by Jacobs.
- The nomination bibliography identifies Cedar Rapids city directories for 1880–1901, 1910, and annually for 1912–1936 among its sources. It does not footnote the Jacobs/Kurik sentence to a particular year or reproduce a deed, lease, partnership filing, or directory entry assigning individual authority.
- The 1906 LOC souvenir predates the 1914 building and produced no Jacobs or Kurik passage. Its Bohemian-community material supplies context only.

### Discovery-only or unresolved leads

- The nomination's source list points to annual 1912–1936 Cedar Rapids directories and the Cedar Rapids Historical Archives Collection. Those record classes could verify duration and exact listing language, but even repeated listings would not by themselves prove title, capital, liability, or lease-signing authority.
- No inspectable deed/tax chain, partnership agreement, probate inventory, lease, debt instrument, supplier account, or court filing assigning authority or consequence to Jacobs individually was recovered.

### Decision

**BLOCKED**

The new inspectable nomination validates that Jacobs and Kurik jointly operated the dry-goods store and collectively leased space to Lesinger. It does not satisfy the queue's person-level gate because it assigns neither the lease nor ownership, liability, capital, or consequential operating authority to Jacobs individually, and it supplies no attributable consequence. Kurik's equal textual position must remain intact.

**Smallest next intervention:** obtain the 1914 Linn County deed and tax/construction chain for 1121 Third Street SE, then inspect any Jacobs & Kurik partnership filing, signed Lesinger lease, probate inventory, or supplier/debt instrument. No Chapter 7 Research successor was created.

## EQ-010 — Caroline Sinclair / Fortunes and Obligations

### Gate tested

Inspectable evidence must show Caroline's authority over the capital commitment plus attributable exposure or consequence. Ownership, commissioning, expense, and construction oversight strengthen agency but do not alone prove source/control of funds, accepted downside, or a consequence borne by Caroline.

### Inspectable sources and identifiers

1. **Brucemore, official history page** — inspected directly.
   - https://www.brucemore.org/history/
2. **Brucemore, “The Sinclair Era Mansion”** — inspected directly.
   - https://www.brucemore.org/history/sinclair-era-mansion-2/
3. **Brucemore, “The Sinclair Era Architects”** — inspected directly.
   - https://www.brucemore.org/history/sinclair-architects/
4. **The History Center, “The Sinclair Family”** — inspected directly.
   - https://www.historycenter.org/sinclair
5. **Library of Congress HABS item `ia0417`, Brucemore, survey HABS IA-196** — catalog/resources inspected through official LOC JSON; the survey documents the property, not its financing transaction.
   - https://www.loc.gov/item/ia0417/
6. **City-hosted Bohemian Commercial Historic District nomination** — searched as a local-history cross-check; it supplied no Caroline-specific transaction evidence.
   - https://cms8.revize.com/revize/cedarrapids/Community%20Development/Planning/Historic%20Surveys%20and%20Data/nrhp/bcnhd.pdf

### Findings from inspected evidence

- Brucemore's official history says Caroline was the first owner, built the mansion in 1884–1886 for her six children, and oversaw construction while her brother Charles kept T. M. Sinclair & Co. operating. Its FAQ further says the property “was purchased by Caroline herself in 1884.” These statements materially strengthen her property and execution agency.
- The mansion page states that the project totaled **$55,000** and was Cedar Rapids's most expensive building project in 1885. It identifies the February 1886 *Cedar Rapids Republican* description as the “Just Completed Mansion of Mrs. T. M. Sinclair.” This verifies the reported scale and a contemporaneous completion consequence at institutional-summary level.
- Brucemore says Caroline commissioned Maximillian Allardt and, after he returned to Indianapolis because of his daughter's illness, hired Josselyn & Taylor to take over construction. This is a documented client-side change in the execution chain and preserves the architects' separate roles.
- The History Center says Caroline later supported and oversaw construction of the first Sinclair Memorial Chapel at Coe College. It does not disclose the amount, funding instrument, uncertainty, rejected alternative, personal exposure, or adverse consequence for that project.
- None of the inspected sources identifies the source or legal form of the $55,000; Caroline's inheritance, insurance, company shares, dividends, trust powers, mortgage, payment schedule, change orders, guarantees, cost overruns, asset sale, liquidity loss, or household jeopardy. None documents that she perceived or accepted a downside or bore a financial consequence beyond owning, completing, occupying, and later exchanging the property.
- The HABS item documents the surviving place through photographs, drawings, and data pages. Physical documentation cannot establish the missing capital authority, uncertainty, or personal exposure.

### Discovery-only or unresolved leads

- Brucemore's public pages identify a contemporaneous February 1886 *Cedar Rapids Republican* article and institutional collections/archives, but no construction ledger, contract, invoice, or correspondence was publicly linked in the inspected text.
- No inspectable 1884 deed, mortgage, T. M. Sinclair probate inventory, will, trust, insurance record, company distribution, construction account, or 1906 exchange instrument was recovered.

### Decision

**BLOCKED**

The newly inspected Brucemore pages strengthen Caroline's agency substantially: she purchased the property herself, commissioned the original architect, hired replacement architects, oversaw construction, completed a $55,000 project, and occupied it. The queue nevertheless requires authority over the relevant funds plus accepted exposure or consequence. The public institutional syntheses do not show what funds she controlled, what downside she accepted, or what financial/personal consequence she bore. Cost and ownership cannot be treated as proof of personally verified risk.

**Smallest next intervention:** obtain the 1884 deed and mortgage/release chain plus T. M. Sinclair's probate inventory/will and Caroline's construction contracts or account ledger from Linn County and the Brucemore/Sinclair family papers. A signed contract or ledger must be linked to her legal funding authority and an attributable overrun, debt, asset conversion, loss, or other consequence. No Chapter 10 Research successor was created.
