### Findings from inspected evidence

- The 1907 *Street Railway Journal* establishes that **Isaac B. Smith was traffic manager** of the Cedar Rapids & Iowa City Railway & Light Company and presented on joint operation of city and interurban cars over city tracks. That is attributable traffic/operating responsibility, not capital authorization.
- The same journal describes the Cedar Rapids–Iowa City road as entering freight service when operations began in 1904, using a baggage car for less-than-carload freight and an electric locomotive for carloads. It also reports the company's 1906 gross earnings, operating expenses, and net earnings. These facts establish operations and corporate consequence, not who approved the corridor or assumed responsibility for its capital.
- The *Street Railway Review* lists **W. J. Greene of Cedar Rapids** among contributors assigned to discuss “Effect of a Day Load on Station Economy.” This is stronger evidence that Greene participated in contemporary power-station economy work, but it does not identify his corporate title in that passage or attribute a boiler purchase, bond, franchise, corridor, or power-capacity authorization to him.
- The December 1907 *Power Engineering* and *Electrical World* OCR inspected in this pass did not yield the queue's required person-specific Cedar Rapids authorization chain. Absence from OCR is not proof that no relevant printed passage exists; OCR errors and incomplete issue selection remain possible.
- No inspected board resolution, signed annual report, bond instrument, purchase order, contract, franchise proceeding, ordinance, right-of-way instrument, or ICC finding tied Greene, Smith, Pinney, Severa, Dows, or another named person to the required specific capital decision and accepted consequence.

### Inaccessible or discovery-only leads

- A city historic-resources search result described 1903 restructuring “under the new leadership of W. G. Dows.” The underlying city-hosted document was not recovered as an inspectable transaction or board record in this pass. Even if inspected, “leadership” alone would not establish authorization of the interurban or boiler commitment.
- The *Street Railway Journal* notes that an ICC complaint by the Cedar Rapids & Iowa City Railway against the Chicago & Northwestern over through routes and joint rates was awaiting decision. That is an inspected regulatory lead about freight access; it does not establish the earlier capital authorizer. The underlying ICC docket/order was not recovered here.

### Decision

**BLOCKED**

The new inspectable material strengthens role allocation: Smith had traffic responsibility, Greene participated in station-economy discussion, and the company had measurable freight operations and earnings. It still does not name the person who authorized a specific boiler, generation, financing, franchise, right-of-way, or interurban capital commitment and accepted its consequence.

**Smallest next intervention:** obtain the 1903–1907 corporate minute book or a signed bond/purchase/franchise instrument from the Cedar Rapids & Iowa City Railway & Light Company (or its legal predecessor), then inspect the full ICC docket for party testimony identifying corporate authority. No Chapter 12 Research successor was created.

## EQ-020 — Milo Barta / Labor Institution

### Gate tested

Barta-specific evidence must show knowingly accepted employment, livelihood, standing, retaliation, or other personal downside, or an attributable consequence. The danger faced by workers generally and the fact of becoming a named founder do not alone pass.

### Inspectable sources and identifiers

1. **University of Iowa ArchivesSpace, Iowa Labor History Oral Project (ILHOP), collection MsZ71**.
   - Collection: https://aspace.lib.uiowa.edu/repositories/2/resources/4195
2. **Barta, Milo — Packinghouse Worker**, archival object 930721, dated November 30, 1977.
   - https://aspace.lib.uiowa.edu/repositories/2/archival_objects/930721
3. **Barta, Milo — Audio**, archival object 930722, dated November 30, 1977.
   - https://aspace.lib.uiowa.edu/repositories/2/archival_objects/930722
4. **Barta, Milo — Transcript**, archival object 930723, dated November 30, 1977.
   - https://aspace.lib.uiowa.edu/repositories/2/archival_objects/930723

### Findings from inspected evidence

- The ArchivesSpace hierarchy verifies that the University of Iowa holds a Barta interview in ILHOP and classifies him as a **packinghouse worker**. It separately catalogs an audio item and a transcript, all dated November 30, 1977.
- This materially improves the target: the record class sought by the queue exists, and the public catalog ties Barta to packinghouse work rather than merely naming him in later synthesis.
- The public item pages provide descriptive metadata, not interview content. The transcript page states “No requestable containers” in the public interface and does not expose transcript text. Therefore, no statement by Barta about organizing, fear, job status in August 1933, employer response, wages, firing, demotion, blacklisting, family dependence, or another accepted consequence was inspected.

### Inaccessible or discovery-only leads

- Iowa Digital Library node 245304 is indexed as “Milo Barta oral history interview, November 1977,” interview by Paul Kelso, in the Iowa Labor History Oral Project: https://digital.lib.uiowa.edu/node/245304
- Direct browser and HTTP inspection returned **403 Forbidden**. Its audio/transcript content was not inspected and supplies no usable risk evidence in this pass.
- General web discovery surfaced biographical name collisions for other people named Milo Barta. Those results were not used to identify the interviewee or infer employment/risk.

### Decision

**BLOCKED**

The inspected catalog proves that a highly relevant Barta-specific oral history, audio item, and transcript exist and identifies him as a packinghouse worker. It does not reveal whether Barta knowingly accepted a personal downside or suffered a consequence. Catalog existence and occupation are insufficient to convert structural exposure into personal risk.

**Smallest next intervention:** obtain the transcript or audio for ArchivesSpace objects 930722–930723 from University of Iowa Special Collections and inspect the passages on the 1933 founding, Barta's job status, management reaction, and consequences. If those passages are silent, inspect Wilson personnel/payroll records keyed to Barta. No Chapter 20 Research successor was created.

## EQ-026 — James Faulkes / Moving Market

### Gate tested

The pass condition requires (1) verified identity and (2) an attributable development decision with accepted exposure. A name attached to co-owned land is insufficient.

### Findings from inspected evidence

- The Gazette article says Younkers and Sears announced a $10 million, seventy-two-acre, fifty-store open-air center in February 1956.
- It says the cornfield-and-timber site belonged to Cedar Rapids residents **James Faulkes and Malcom Thompson**. It does not provide Faulkes's middle name, age, occupation, address, deed reference, parcel, ownership share, probate link, or other identity bridge to James Nelson Faulkes.
- It credits **Bernard Greenbaum & Associates** with beginning construction in September 1959 and identifies the opening on September 15, 1960, with thirty stores and three anchors. This preserves a divided production chain rather than making Faulkes the developer by association.
- The article's wording does not establish whether Faulkes sold, leased, optioned, contributed, or retained the land; invested capital; joined a development entity; signed debt or guarantees; selected Greenbaum; negotiated with anchors or tenants; approved plans; or bore a documented consequence.
- No deed, corporate filing, partnership agreement, mortgage, development contract, lease, site plan, applicant record, or identity record was recovered and inspected in this pass.

### Inaccessible or discovery-only leads

- The five Newspapers.com IDs above are article-linked page leads. Their page images/OCR were not accessible, so they cannot be used to infer identity, authority, lease terms, or exposure.
- General search and Internet Archive/Google Books discovery produced no inspectable deed or corporate record connecting a fully identified Faulkes to Lindale's development entity. “No result in this pass” is not evidence that no such record exists.

### Decision

**BLOCKED**

The strongest inspected source still establishes only site association/co-ownership with Thompson and separately attributes construction to Greenbaum. It does not verify which James Faulkes was involved or assign him a development decision and accepted downside.

**Smallest next intervention:** inspect the Linn County recorder's 1955–1960 grantor/grantee and mortgage indexes for James Faulkes, Malcom/Malcolm Thompson, Lindale Plaza, and the Greenbaum entity; obtain the resulting deed/option/lease instrument plus the development entity's Iowa corporate filing or anchor lease. No Chapter 26 Research successor was created.
