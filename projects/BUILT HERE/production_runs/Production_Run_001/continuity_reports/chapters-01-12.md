## Repeated-context and voice findings across the section

1. **The dominant repeated lesson is “form is not function.”** Chapters 1, 2, 5, and 6 repeatedly explain that a visible form—plat, interest, rail, or lot—does not make the operating system work. The motif is architecturally useful, but each chapter should own one distinct version:
   - Chapter 1: definition makes entry possible.
   - Chapter 2: entry accepts inherited relational exposure.
   - Chapter 5: fixed infrastructure still requires collective operation.
   - Chapter 6: public promotion tests an outer proposition before local systems converge.
2. **The dominant sentence pattern is corrective antithesis.** “Not X, but Y,” “did not prove,” and “cannot be claimed” protect evidence but recur often enough to slow narrative momentum. Preserve material qualifications once, then carry them through exact nouns and verbs.
3. **Chapter endings are structurally strong but visibly schematic.** The exact bold conditions successfully prevent false personal handoffs. They currently read as production controls rather than finished narrative. Preserve them through development; address format only in the authorized one-author line edit.
4. **Source-method language is heaviest in Chapters 1–2 and 11.** It is generally responsible, but repeated statements about unrecovered records can make the reader experience the archive rather than the decision. Qualify each material issue once where possible.
