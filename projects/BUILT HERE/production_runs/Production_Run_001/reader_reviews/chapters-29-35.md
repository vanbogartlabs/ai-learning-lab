## Chapter 30 — Saving Memory, Rebuilding Place

### Verdict: **FAIL — Major reader-experience revision required**

### What the reader wants

The opening creates a strong question: how can Naughton preserve one institution when its building, collections, programs, offices, money, and construction must move on different clocks? The reader wants to see that paradox tighten through concrete consequences and converge in the moved building and July 2012 reopening.

The chapter currently keeps interrupting that desire with renewed explanation of the same system. The mechanism is established by:

> “A damaged institution does not rebuild as one object.”

It is then restated through “several clocks,” “different sequences,” “two levels,” “different workstreams,” “unlike units,” “one stalled track,” “recombination,” and the warning that one advance must not “masquerade as return.” These are compatible ideas, but their accumulation flattens escalation.

### Reader-test assessment

- **Decision clarity — PASS:** Naughton's defining choice is clear: keep mission and public function active while coordinating separate recovery paths toward a permanent return. The chapter does not steal Chapter 29's blocked emergency decision.
- **Emotional/courage stakes — FAIL at Major severity:** Professional, donor, staff, community, and reputational stakes are named, but they are mostly presented as categories. The map in thirty-two pieces, displaced public programming, responsibility for a campaign above $28 million, and the visible movement of the building could carry the pressure more directly. Instead, interpretive guardrails repeatedly arrive before consequence has time to land.
- **Comprehension — PASS:** The reader can understand Naughton's executive role and the distribution of credit. The role boundary is careful and clear.
- **Trust — PASS:** Qualifications are honest; Naughton is not converted into owner, engineer, conservator, or sole savior. The conflicting move/elevation measurements are handled responsibly.
- **Pacing/repetition — FAIL at Major severity:** The second half repeatedly re-proves separate schedules, distributed authority, and bounded reopening. The chapter reaches its physical and organizational consequence, then circles the mechanism again before the exact handoff.
- **Memorable truth — PASS:** “The museum saved the building by moving it and saved institutional wholeness by letting its parts recover separately” is strong, specific, and retellable.
- **Cedar Rapids reciprocity — PASS:** The city supplies people, temporary space, public attention, planning, construction capacity, and funding networks; the museum returns an adaptable preservation method and continued public cultural function. The exchange is bounded rather than celebratory.
- **Chronology/forward pull — PASS with a reader seam:** Post-damage entry and July 2012 reopening are clear. The final exact inheritance points forward, but the near-duplicate paragraph before it makes the transition feel appended rather than earned.

### Exact revision instructions

1. **Preserve unchanged:** the opening-page paradox; Chapter 29 boundary; Naughton's executive-not-owner role; distributed credit; the evidence qualification around the conservation details and move/elevation measurements; the more-than-$28-million attribution; 30,000-square-foot addition; July 2012 bounded reopening; Cedar Rapids reciprocity; and the exact final sentence.
2. **Move the already present material detail forward:** after the first explanation that a damaged institution does not rebuild as one object, bring in the frozen books / cleaned records / map in thirty-two pieces paragraph. Use it as the concrete proof that one recovery clock could not govern the institution. Do not add scene detail or remove its publication-level qualification.
3. **Compress the middle mechanism:** retain one paragraph establishing separate workstreams, one paragraph on temporary public function through *Rising Above*, one paragraph on differentiated capital/accountability, and one paragraph on distributed authority. Combine or delete later sentences whose sole function is to restate that programs, collections, capital, and construction moved on different schedules.
4. **Make the decision recur as action, not terminology:** at the $28 million, building move, expansion, and reopening turns, state what new obligation or convergence each development created. Remove repeated definitions of “leadership,” “dependence,” “separation,” and “recombination” when the concrete consequence already demonstrates them.
5. **Tighten the close:** after the July 2012 reopening, retain one consequence paragraph and one courage paragraph. Rewrite the penultimate paragraph so it lands on Naughton's bounded consequence and does not paraphrase both halves of the outgoing handoff. Keep the authoritative lowercase final sentence byte-for-byte as the final nonblank line.
6. **Acceptance test:** a reader should encounter a visible escalation—fragmented materials and displaced mission → public continuity → capital and physical redesign → recombination and reopening—without receiving more than one full explanation of the “many clocks” mechanism in each stage.

## Chapter 31 — The Market in the Factory

### Verdict: **FAIL — Major reader-experience revision required**

The reader then wants to know whether Ordover can secure the Quality Chef site, preserve the buildings, make the public case under explicit fear of failure, and create a place that later merchants can actually enter.

Instead, the chapter repeatedly translates each stage back into platform theory. The result is lucid but over-mediated.

- **Comprehension — PASS:** The reader understands nonprofit board authority, City control, consultants, funders, builders, operators, and merchant responsibilities. The plan-versus-completed-form boundary is clear.
- **Trust — PASS:** Proposed funding is not treated as received money; Ordover is not made sole founder or owner; Poe and the coalition retain independent agency.
- **Pacing/repetition — FAIL at Major severity:** “Site first,” “risk moved upstream,” “platform not project,” “smaller entry did not remove uncertainty,” and “completion cannot be projected backward” recur after the reader has understood them. The opening-period numbers arrive late and are followed by another extended synthesis.
- **Memorable truth — PASS:** “The stall-sized bet required a building-sized wager upstream” is the strongest reader-retell truth in the chapter.
- **Cedar Rapids reciprocity — PASS:** The city provides a flood-affected site, public negotiation, possible in-kind support, local vendors, volunteers, and funding networks; the nonprofit returns adaptive reuse, shared commercial infrastructure, gathering space, and lower-threshold entry. Public scrutiny and taxpayer exposure prevent the exchange from feeling one-sided.

Remove the production-facing sentence “Nothing personal or corporate passes forward from NewBo; only the bounded city condition remains, before any unvalidated operating decision is attached to it.” Express that boundary in reader-facing prose without naming production validation, then keep the exact terminal condition byte-for-byte and final.
