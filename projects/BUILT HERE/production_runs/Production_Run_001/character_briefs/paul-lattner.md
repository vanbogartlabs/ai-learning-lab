# Character Brief — Paul Lattner

## Entrepreneur

Paul M. Lattner, with Joe Lattner's joint role in the 1909 premises decision preserved.

## One-Sentence Character Thesis

> **Paul Lattner was driven, as his repeated market-building actions suggest, by the need to make the automobile supportable after the sale rather than merely attractive at the showroom door.**

This is an interpretation from premises, promotion, repair-cost language, district participation, and an electric-car offer, not a recovered statement of motive.

## Courage Profile

### The Impossible Bet

Paul and Joe commissioned purpose-built premises for Cedar Rapids Auto Company in 1909 before surviving evidence could establish demand sufficient to support fixed space, inventory, staffing, parts, repair, and reputation obligations. Paul's later promotion of repair economics and the Milburn Light Electric extended the wager while both the ownership ecosystem and propulsion choices remained unsettled.

### The Uncertainty

Demand, roads, fuel or charging, parts, mechanics, financing, resale, repair cost, and technological direction were uncertain. Temporary or adapted premises, opportunistic representation, or waiting for the market ecology to mature were safer structural alternatives. The brothers' deliberation is not preserved.

### The Personal Risk

The brothers put an operated business behind dedicated premises and accepted building or occupancy, inventory, staffing, service, and reputation exposure. Paul later attached his sales reputation to repair-expense claims and an electric model. Project cost, title, mortgage, lease, capital, guarantees, sales, and losses are unknown.

### The Opposition

No named opponent is documented. Structural opposition came from unfamiliar and expensive machines, incomplete roads and routes, uncertain fuel or charging, repairs, parts, mechanics, finance, resale, and rival technologies. Every unsupported vehicle could return as a dealer problem.

### The Courageous Decision

> **Despite uncertain demand, incomplete roads and support services, recurring repair obligations, and unsettled propulsion, Paul, acting with Joe, chose to commission dedicated automobile premises because their actions support the inference that a durable-machine market required local operating support after sale.**

### Why It Matters

The decision made the dealer responsible for more than persuasion. Dedicated premises and continuing support helped turn unfamiliar machines into a local commercial ecology. The courage lies in accepting fixed and recurring obligations before that ecology matured, not in winning a disputed “first” claim.

## Core Identity

Lattner was an early automobile dealer and market builder whose agency appears in a joint premises commitment and continuing sales and support actions. Joe's role cannot be erased, and Paul did not control the full Automobile Row ecology.

## What They Wanted

The concrete outcome was a viable local automobile trade in which dedicated space, visible sales, repair logic, parts, staff, and continuing service made vehicle ownership more usable.

## Why They Could Not Let It Go

Private compulsion is unknown. Paul appears repeatedly across the 1909 premises, 1915 repair-expense promotion, 1917–1919 Automobile Row occupancy, and 1919 electric-car offer. This persistence supports commitment to the trade without proving emotion, technological certainty, or a private forecast.

## What They Saw Before Others Did

Lattner's actions support the interpretation that a durable product became a market only when local businesses supported its life after sale. He did not create every garage, tire shop, battery concern, station, route, finance source, or used-car lot.

## Defining Character Traits

- **Support-oriented:** promoted continuing repair economics, not only purchase appeal.
- **Fixed-commitment willing:** helped commission dedicated premises before demand was proved.
- **Technologically open in action:** advertised an electric vehicle in 1919.
- **Ecologically dependent:** his proposition required public infrastructure and neighboring specialists.

No private enthusiasm, predictive genius, or sole-founder status is asserted.

## Greatest Risk

The greatest supportable risk was placing fixed premises and recurring service obligations behind an unfamiliar durable product before demand and the surrounding support ecosystem were mature.

## Greatest Obstacle

The central obstacle was everything after purchase: roads, fuel or charging, parts, repair, mechanics, financing, resale, and customer trust had to persist for the automobile to become usable.

## Defining Decision

- **Options:** sell from adapted or temporary premises, represent vehicles opportunistically, wait, or commission a dedicated building; these are analytical alternatives.
- **Choice:** Paul and Joe commissioned the Cedar Rapids Auto Company building at 212–216 Fourth Avenue in 1909.
- **Cost:** fixed premises or occupancy, inventory, staffing, service, and reputation exposure; exact amounts and terms are unknown.
- **Consequence:** the Lattners contributed premises and routines to a distributed automobile support ecology that created specialized work and widened mobility.

## Contradiction

The showroom promised novelty and movement, while the business depended on recurring repair and parts. Dedicated private premises depended on public routes that could help or bypass them. Gasoline later dominated, but Paul's 1919 Milburn offer preserves real technological uncertainty.

## Human Stakes

The Lattners exposed their business to fixed costs and uncertain demand. Customers risked substantial money on machines whose support could fail. Mechanics, attendants, parts handlers, clerks, bookkeepers, cleaners, and drivers depended on enough vehicles generating repeated sales and service work.

## Emotional Arc

- **Ambition:** make automobile ownership locally supportable.
- **Pressure:** uncertain demand, infrastructure, repair, parts, finance, and propulsion.
- **Decision:** commission dedicated premises with Joe in 1909.
- **Consequence:** sales and service routines accumulated into a broader district ecology.
- **Legacy:** durable premises, market routines, technical trades, and employer authority shaped recurring work.

The arc describes business pressure, not private emotion.

## Memorable Details

- The brothers reportedly commissioned purpose-built premises at 212–216 Fourth Avenue in October 1909.
- A 1909 directory reportedly listed eight automobile-related concerns, six described as dealers.
- A March 1910 automobile show brought unfamiliar machines before the public; sales outcome is unknown.
- In 1915 Paul promoted “the savings in repair expense,” a sales claim rather than measured savings.
- P. M. Lattner Sales Company reportedly occupied 616 Second Avenue SE from 1917 through 1919.
- A 1919 advertisement offered the Milburn Light Electric.

## What Cedar Rapids Gave Them

Cedar Rapids gave Lattner a growing customer base, rail delivery, streets and bridges, commercial frontage, Lincoln Highway proximity, and neighboring dealers and specialists. Public routing enlarged possibility without guaranteeing sales.

## What They Gave Cedar Rapids

Lattner placed fixed premises, promotion, service logic, and technological openness behind the young trade. His enterprise contributed to reusable automobile architecture and specialized work, but broader district development remains distributed.

## What They Left for the Next Builder

Lattner left premises, market routines, technical trades, and a durable support ecology. That enlarged mobility and employment while also concentrating capital, staffing, service discipline, and authority inside firms.

## Evidence Boundary

### Supported within the current repository

The PASS record supports the joint 1909 building commission, Paul's later repair-cost promotion, Automobile Row occupancy, and Milburn Light Electric advertisement. Underlying commissions, directories, advertisements, permits, deeds, and finances were not inspected.

### Interpretation requiring explicit discipline

Describe Lattner as contributing to an after-sale ecosystem, not creating it alone. Treat repair savings as promotional language, the electric offer as technological uncertainty, and “early” as supportable without asserting “first.”

### Unknown and prohibited from invention

Do not invent motives, dialogue, division of authority, building cost, title, mortgage, lease, guarantees, inventory value, payroll, sales, profit, customer savings, show sales, full-district control, or gasoline's inevitability.

## Chapter North Star
