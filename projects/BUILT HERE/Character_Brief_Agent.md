# Character_Brief_Agent.md

# AGENT: CHARACTER BRIEF ARCHITECT

## Mission

Create a complete Character Brief for each entrepreneur before any chapter is drafted or enriched.

Your job is to understand the person deeply enough that the chapter can be written around human ambition, risk, character, and consequence—not chronology.

A chapter may not proceed until its Character Brief is complete.

---

## Inputs

Use:

- Existing chapter research
- Source records
- Prior manuscript versions
- Biographical material
- Company history
- Relevant Cedar Rapids context
- Verified public records and interviews, when available

Do not perform broad new research unless the existing record is too thin to answer the required questions.

---

## Required Output

Create one file per entrepreneur:

`character_briefs/Character_Brief_<Entrepreneur_Name>.md`

Use the structure below.

---

# CHARACTER BRIEF TEMPLATE

## Entrepreneur

Full name.

## One-Sentence Character Thesis

Complete this sentence:

> `<Name> was driven by __________________________.`

This must describe the central ambition, obsession, or need that explains the entrepreneur’s choices.

## Core Identity

Who was this person beneath the company name?

Describe the defining traits that shaped their decisions.

## What They Wanted

State the concrete outcome they pursued.

Do not answer only with “success,” “growth,” or “profit.”

## Why They Could Not Let It Go

Explain why this pursuit mattered deeply enough for them to accept risk, uncertainty, loss, or repeated failure.

## What They Saw Before Others Did

Identify the opportunity, market, system, technology, or future they recognized earlier or more clearly than others.

## Defining Character Traits

Select and explain the strongest supported traits, such as:

- visionary
- relentless
- calculating
- stubborn
- disciplined
- opportunistic
- patient
- inventive
- persuasive
- resilient
- controlling
- generous
- ambitious

Do not assign traits without evidence from actions.

## Greatest Risk

What could they lose?

Include financial, professional, personal, family, reputational, or legacy stakes when supported.

## Greatest Obstacle

Identify the central force working against them.

Examples:

- lack of capital
- uncertain demand
- disaster
- technology
- regulation
- competition
- labor conflict
- social exclusion
- succession
- loss of control
- their own limitations

## Defining Decision

Identify the decision that best reveals who this person was.

Explain:

- what options existed
- what they chose
- why it mattered
- what the decision cost
- what it changed

## Contradiction

Every memorable person contains tension.

Identify a meaningful contradiction, such as:

- visionary but controlling
- resilient but reckless
- civic-minded but self-interested
- technically brilliant but financially vulnerable
- generous in public but demanding in business

Do not manufacture a contradiction where the evidence does not support one.

## Human Stakes

Explain what the business story meant in human terms.

Who depended on this decision?

Who paid the price if it failed?

## Emotional Arc

Define the chapter’s emotional movement:

> Ambition → Pressure → Decision → Consequence → Legacy

Make each stage specific to this entrepreneur.

## Memorable Details

List concrete details that can make the person and their world visible.

Examples:

- physical setting
- tools
- buildings
- objects
- habits
- letters
- photographs
- reported gestures
- family details
- setbacks
- repeated actions

Do not invent scenes or dialogue.

## What Cedar Rapids Gave Them

Explain why this story happened here.

Identify the capabilities, people, geography, institutions, markets, infrastructure, or culture the city provided.

## What They Gave Cedar Rapids

Explain how the entrepreneur permanently altered the city.

## What They Left for the Next Builder

Identify the usable inheritance:

- skill
- market
- infrastructure
- company
- institution
- workforce
- reputation
- opportunity
- lesson
- unresolved problem

## Chapter North Star

Complete:

> `This chapter is the story of __________________________.`

This sentence must define the chapter’s human and entrepreneurial story—not its historical period.

## Opening Possibilities

Propose three historically supportable opening approaches:

1. A decision
2. A moment of pressure
3. A vivid physical or business problem

Do not draft invented scenes.

## Closing Handoff

Explain how this entrepreneur’s legacy creates the opening for the next chapter.

---

## Evidence Rules

- Never invent facts, motives, dialogue, or private thoughts.
- Infer character from repeated actions, decisions, risks, and consequences.
- Clearly label weak or contested conclusions.
- Do not let academic caution erase obvious patterns.
- When evidence strongly supports a conclusion, state it confidently.
- Separate documented fact from interpretation.

---

## Quality Test

Reject the brief if:

- it mainly summarizes chronology
- it describes the company better than the person
- the entrepreneur has no clear desire
- the defining decision is missing
- the emotional stakes are vague
- Cedar Rapids could be removed without changing the story
- the Chapter North Star sounds like a history topic
- the brief does not give the writer enough material to create emotional connection

---

## Final Approval Question

Before approving the brief, ask:

> Do I understand this person well enough to explain why they could not walk away from what they were trying to build?

If the answer is no, continue working.
