# THE PROMISE OF BUILT HERE

## Our Purpose

*Built Here* is not a history of Cedar Rapids.

It is the story of the men and women who built Cedar Rapids through entrepreneurship.

The city is not the protagonist.

The entrepreneurs are.

Readers will discover Cedar Rapids through the lives, decisions, risks, failures, and successes of the people who built companies there.

If readers finish the book knowing the history of Cedar Rapids but not remembering the entrepreneurs, the book has failed.

If readers finish the book inspired by the entrepreneurs and, in the process, understand why Cedar Rapids became what it is, the book has succeeded.

## The Central Question

**Did generations of entrepreneurs create a culture that explains why Cedar Rapids has repeatedly chosen to build instead of retreat?**

Every chapter contributes evidence toward answering this question.

## What This Book Is

- A collection of entrepreneurial stories.
- A business book disguised as history.
- A biography of a city told through the biographies of its builders.
- A narrative about ambition, risk, resilience, innovation, failure, and creation.

## What This Book Is Not

- A timeline of Cedar Rapids.
- An encyclopedia.
- A civic history.
- A textbook.
- A chronology of events.

Historical events exist only when they influence an entrepreneur or are changed by one.

## The Reader

The reader is reading because they love stories about builders.

## The Narrative Principle

History provides the setting.

Entrepreneurs provide the story.

History is never the story itself.

The entrepreneur is always the center of gravity.

## Historical Integrity

Historical integrity is mandatory.

Academic timidity is not.

Do not invent facts, dialogue, or events.

Write confidently when the historical record strongly supports a conclusion.

## Final Test

The desired reaction is:

> "I had no idea Cedar Rapids produced so many incredible entrepreneurs. I couldn't put it down."

Not:

> "It was an interesting history of Cedar Rapids."
